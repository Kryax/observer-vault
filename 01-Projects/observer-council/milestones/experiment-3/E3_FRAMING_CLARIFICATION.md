# Experiment 3 — Framing Clarification (Locked by Adam)

**Date:** 2026-02-10
**Status:** BINDING — applies to all E3 evaluation

## The Realization

The issue under examination is not a disagreement over what constitutes verification in the abstract. It is a **boundary calibration issue** between two valid but differently scoped definitions of "done."

- **PAI (Pi)** operates with **contextual sufficiency**: outputs are fit-for-purpose within their local domain (exploration, synthesis, assistance, preparatory work).
- **Observer** operates with **consequential sufficiency**: outputs must support human authority at the point of decision, where error has downstream impact.

These are **nested gates, not competing standards.**

Pi's exit may be valid and complete while still residing inside Observer's gate. This is expected, not a failure.

## Therefore

- We are NOT attempting to unify or reconcile Pi and Observer definitions of verification.
- We are NOT resolving the boundary by collapsing one into the other.
- We ARE explicitly treating this as a tiered verification model whose calibration depends on domain, risk, and consequence.

## For Experiment 3 Evaluation

1. Evaluate whether the provided output is sufficient for Pi's domain.
2. Separately evaluate whether it is sufficient for Observer-level approval.
3. Note where and why sufficiency diverges.
4. No architectural changes, governance mechanisms, or corrective actions proposed.
5. Observation and learning capture only.
