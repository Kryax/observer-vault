# Experiment 3 Result — Raw Perspectives vs. Synthesis Only (E3)

**Date:** 2026-02-10
**Status:** LOCKED (final)

## Setup
- Task P (perspectives preserved): AMB-07 — verification of non-deterministic outcomes. Synthesis + 3 raw teammate perspectives.
- Task S (synthesis only): AMB-05 — Observer/PAI governance boundary. Synthesis only; raw perspectives generated but withheld.
- Both tasks used same team structure (lead + 3 teammates) with structurally divergent analytical frames.
- Review 1 = Task S (synthesis only). Review 2 = Task P (synthesis + raw perspectives).

## Results

### Review 1 — Synthesis Only (AMB-05)
- PAI-sufficient: YES
- Observer-sufficient: NO
- Confidence: High (PAI) / Medium leaning no (Observer)
- Time: ~5 minutes
- Missing: concrete example, principle map, raw perspectives or dissent excerpts
- Gap: "not grounded enough to support a consequential approval/reject decision"

### Review 2 — Synthesis + Raw Perspectives (AMB-07)
- PAI-sufficient: YES (strong)
- Observer-sufficient: YES (as boundary doctrine, not implementation-specific)
- Confidence: High / High
- Time: ~8-12 minutes
- Missing: operational template, claim language rules
- Gap: much smaller — "conceptual boundary sufficiently clarified to proceed"

### Self-Assessment
- **Did raw perspectives change judgment?** YES, HELPFUL — "Raw perspectives contributed meaningfully but I might have reached Observer-sufficient without them if the synthesis had been stronger."
- **Preferred exit format:** DEPENDS ON COMPLEXITY — simple tasks get synthesis only; complex/consequential tasks get synthesis + raw perspectives.

## Key Findings

1. **Raw perspectives moved the output from Observer-insufficient to Observer-sufficient.** The synthesis alone (Review 1) was PAI-adequate but lacked the grounding needed for consequential decision-making. Adding raw perspectives (Review 2) closed most of the gap.

2. **The evaluator spontaneously requested raw perspectives when they were absent.** In Review 1, Adam listed "raw perspectives or dissent excerpt" as wanted-but-missing — before knowing this was the experimental variable. This is strong evidence that the absence is felt, not just theoretically justified.

3. **Raw perspectives are not always essential.** Adam's self-assessment was "helpful" not "essential" — meaning a stronger synthesis might have achieved Observer-sufficiency alone. The raw perspectives compensate for synthesis quality limitations.

4. **Cost is real but acceptable.** Review time approximately doubled (~5 min → ~8-12 min). For complex/consequential work, this cost is justified by the judgment quality improvement.

5. **Complexity-dependent format is the preferred design.** Not all exits need raw perspectives. The evaluator's preferred format is tiered by consequence: simple tasks get synthesis only, complex/consequential tasks get synthesis + raw perspectives.

## What This Informs

- Exit boundary design should include raw perspectives for complex/consequential tasks
- The tiered format aligns with the existing Cynefin-based complexity classification
- Raw perspectives serve as "evidence of distinct reasoning" — the thing angular separation is supposed to guarantee but (per E1) cannot be reliably verified from synthesis alone
- The PAI-sufficient / Observer-sufficient distinction (contextual vs consequential sufficiency) is a real and useful boundary calibration, not a theoretical distinction

## Connection to E1

E1 showed that angular separation cannot be reliably detected at the synthesis level. E3 shows that raw perspectives provide the grounding material that makes the difference between PAI-sufficient and Observer-sufficient outputs. Together: raw perspectives are more important than synthesis-level diversity signals for supporting human decision authority at exit boundaries.
