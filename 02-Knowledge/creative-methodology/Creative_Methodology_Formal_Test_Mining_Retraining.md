## PHASE 1: OscillateAndGenerate

### Oscillation Log

#### Cycle 1
- **Boundary:** Australia's mining workforce (~270,000 direct employees) needs a retraining architecture to transition skills from fossil fuel extraction (coal, iron ore processing) to critical minerals (lithium, rare earths, cobalt) within existing regulatory, geographic, and institutional constraints.
- **Components:** Individual miner skill profiles (blast certification, heavy vehicle operation, geological sampling, ventilation management, processing plant operation) | TAFE system capacity and curriculum timelines | FIFO logistics networks (Perth-Pilbara, Townsville-Bowen Basin, Adelaide-Olympic Dam) | State mining regulations (WA DMP, QLD RSHQ, SA DEM) | Company training infrastructure (BHP Academy, Rio Tinto centres, Fortescue programs) | Union training agreements (CFMEU Mining & Energy) | Critical minerals processing knowledge gaps (hydrometallurgy, solvent extraction, flotation chemistry for spodumene) | Age demographics (~42 average, 15-20yr career runway) | Community dependency (Moranbah, Collie, Muswellbrook) | Federal funding mechanisms (National Skills Commission) | Existing transferable skills inventory | Mine closure vs. new mine ramp-up timelines
- **Boundary Shift:** Initial boundary. Already noticing "retraining" may understate the challenge — many skills transfer directly, so the architecture is partly a reclassification and gap-filling exercise, not a wholesale rebuild.

#### Cycle 2
- **Boundary:** Not primarily a retraining system but a skill-mapping, gap-identification, and targeted upskilling system operating across three distinct transition corridors: (1) coal-to-critical-minerals in QLD/NSW, (2) iron-ore-adjacent lithium expansion in WA, (3) greenfield rare earth/copper in SA — each with different skill transfer ratios, geographic constraints, and timeline pressures.
- **Components:** Corridor 1 (QLD/NSW): Hunter Valley coal miners → potential lithium/cobalt processing (geographic mismatch — deposits in WA), Bowen Basin open-cut → vanadium (close proximity, high transfer), QLD TAFE network needs modules not new programs, Collinsville vanadium as pilot, CFMEU Hunter Valley political dynamics. Corridor 2 (WA): Greenbushes lithium drawing from Pilbara iron ore, Pilbara Minerals already recruiting from iron ore, processing gap (crush-screen-ship → beneficiation + chemical conversion), Kwinana lithium hydroxide refineries need industrial chemistry skills, WA TAFE needs hydrometallurgy modules, FIFO network repurposable. Corridor 3 (SA): Olympic Dam copper/uranium with rare earth byproduct expansion, new projects (Leigh Creek, Whyalla) with limited existing workforce, SA TAFE needs federal support. Cross-corridor: National credentials framework, micro-credentials vs. full certificates, company poaching dynamics, wage differentials ($150-180K coal vs. potentially less in early-stage minerals), Aboriginal employment obligations.
- **Boundary Shift:** Major reshape. "Australia's mining workforce" is not one workforce — it's three geographically and industrially distinct corridors with different skill transfer ratios. QLD/NSW coal corridor has a geographic mismatch. WA has highest natural transfer but a processing chemistry gap. SA is greenfield, not transition.

#### Cycle 3
- **Boundary:** A federated, corridor-specific system with national coordination, addressing three structural problems: (1) geographic mismatch between coal workers and mineral deposits, (2) processing chemistry knowledge gap separating extraction from refining, (3) wage/conditions gap making voluntary transition economically irrational without bridge mechanisms.
- **Components:** Geographic mismatch: Relocation packages, FIFO reconfiguration (Hunter→WA lithium unlikely without family disruption), in-situ alternative (lithium refining near Hunter Valley port infrastructure), community transition funds. Processing chemistry gap (hardest problem): New domain knowledge — electrochemistry, solvent extraction, reagent handling, crystallization; target roles: process plant operators, metallurgical technicians, lab analysts; cannot be TAFE alone — needs university partnerships (Curtin, UQ, UniSA); simulation/virtual plant environments; apprenticeship in operating refineries (Kwinana, Kemerton) but tiny capacity. Economic rationality gap: Current coal miner $160K, established roster, strong protections vs. potentially lower pay, uncertain timelines, smaller companies; bridge options: wage top-ups, employment insurance, "transition bonds"; superannuation continuity; enterprise bargaining translation. Institutional: National coordinating authority, corridor-specific Training Hubs, digital skills passport, employer co-investment mandate, transition pathway counselors. Temporal sequencing: Phase 1 (0-2yr) skill audit + pilots, Phase 2 (2-5yr) processing chemistry at scale, Phase 3 (5-10yr) full corridor operation + downstream skills.
- **Boundary Shift:** Expanded to include economic rationality — may be the actual binding constraint. Processing chemistry gap requires university partnerships, not just TAFE. Architecture is now three interlocking systems: skills (technical), mobility (geographic/economic), institutions (coordination/credentials).

#### Cycle 4
- **Boundary:** Must also account for: (1) timeline collision between accelerating coal closures and slow critical mineral project approvals, creating a gap period with no jobs at either end; (2) the extraction-vs-downstream fork — training scope doubles if Australia pursues refining, not just mining.
- **Components:** Timeline collision: Hunter Valley closures accelerating (Liddell area transitioning), mineral project approvals 3+ years (Lynas Kalgoorlie precedent), gap risk where workers retrained for non-existent jobs leave industry entirely; buffer via rehabilitation employment, geological survey work, pre-development monitoring; use gap for extended training ("earn while you learn"). Supply chain fork: Extraction-only = moderate skill extension; downstream processing = fundamentally different (industrial chemistry, precision manufacturing, automotive quality systems); current policy push (Future Made in Australia Act) signals downstream but investment pipeline mostly extraction; architecture must be modular. Indigenous dimensions: Deposits on/near Aboriginal land, ILUAs include employment commitments, ~6% Indigenous participation nationally, need co-design not just targets, Ranger mine rehabilitation as model. Automation overlay: Critical minerals designed with higher automation, autonomous haulage standard, net fewer but higher-skilled workers, new domains: autonomous systems supervision, remote ops, sensor/IoT maintenance, data analytics. Identity transition: "Coal miner" is identity not just job, programs ignoring identity disruption fail (Latrobe Valley, UK coalfields precedent), peer mentoring needed, community narrative reframing.
- **Boundary Shift:** Two major additions. Timeline collision is structural — no amount of curriculum fixes it. Extraction-vs-downstream is a fork that doubles training scope. Problem grew from "how to retrain miners" to "how to manage a multi-decade sociotechnical transition."

#### Cycle 5
- **Boundary:** A managed sociotechnical transition system operating under deep uncertainty (policy direction, project timelines, commodity prices, automation rates) while maintaining workforce attachment and community viability. Not a training program but transition infrastructure with training as one component.
- **Components:** Governance: National Critical Minerals Transition Authority — cross-jurisdictional (Murray-Darling Basin Authority model), 15-year mandate with 10-year review. Funding: Tri-lateral: federal appropriation + hypothecated state royalties + employer levy (counter-cyclical). CFMEU as formal partner via union-managed training funds. Skills passport: National database (My Health Record model), maps competencies to AQF, portable across states, machine-readable, privacy-preserving. Six curriculum modules: (1) Lithium processing 12-week at Kwinana/Kemerton with Curtin, (2) Rare earth separation 16-week at Lynas Kalgoorlie with UniSA/UWA, (3) Cobalt/nickel hydrometallurgy 12-week at BHP Nickel West with Murdoch, (4) Autonomous systems 8-week via Perth/Brisbane remote ops, (5) Environmental management for critical minerals 6-week cross-corridor, (6) Downstream manufacturing 20-week contingent on investment. All blended delivery. Gap-period employment: Mine rehabilitation, geological survey, infrastructure construction, environmental monitoring — structured as transition employment with integrated training at mining wages. Risk register: Commodity price crash, political reversal, CFMEU resistance, TAFE capacity strain, company free-riding, regional housing. Success metrics: Worker attachment rate, wage maintenance (≥90% within 3yr), time-to-employment (<12mo), geographic distribution (>60% no permanent relocation), skills passport adoption (>80% in 5yr), Indigenous participation (>6%).
- **Boundary Shift:** Gap-period employment emerged as potentially the pivotal design element — solves timeline, earnings, training time, and useful work simultaneously. Total training duration for downstream pathway: 40-60 weeks per person. This is a 1-2 year commitment, not a short course.

#### Cycle 6
- **Boundary:** National transition infrastructure federated across three corridors, coordinated by national authority with 15-year mandate, funded tri-laterally, CFMEU as formal partner, managing workforce through four-stage pipeline (skills audit → gap-period employment → modular upskilling → wage-bridged placement) via digital skills passports.
- **Components:** Trainer shortage (newly visible): ~200-300 qualified nationally, but only 30-40 with critical minerals-specific operational + teaching capability; university faculty research-focused not vocational; international trainer recruitment geopolitically sensitive; train-the-trainer pipeline 3-5 year lag; simulation/virtual plant environments reduce dependency. Women and diversity: Mining ~17% women concentrated in non-operational; processing plant roles have lower gender barriers; specific recruitment pathways including mining-adjacent roles (admin, logistics, FIFO camp services). Supply chain certification: Tesla, Samsung SDI, EU battery makers require workforce traceability; training architecture produces IRMA-aligned certifications; workforce quality as competitive advantage for exports. National authority detail: Board: federal (chair), state ministers, CFMEU national secretary, MCA, Indigenous rep, TAFE Directors Australia. Functions: maintain skills passport, accredit hubs, manage transition employment, administer levy, commission evaluation. Does NOT deliver training. Regional offices at Hunter Valley, Mackay, Perth, Kalgoorlie, Port Augusta. Individual worker journey (end-to-end): Week 0: closure notification → Week 1-2: skills audit → Week 2-4: transition counselor + family consultation → Week 4+: gap-period employment with evening/weekend theory → Week 16-40: intensive training block → Week 40-44: placement with wage bridge → Ongoing: skills passport, mentoring, 12-month check-in.
- **Boundary Shift:** Trainer shortage (30-40 people for critical minerals specifically) is the binding bottleneck. Supply chain certification reframes training from cost to revenue driver. Individual worker journey reveals 40-week minimum critical path, meaning pipeline capacity of ~5,000/year needed for 10% of workforce — which circles back to trainer shortage. Boundary now stable.

### Oscillation Summary
- **Cycles completed:** 6
- **Final boundary:** A national, federated transition infrastructure across three corridors (QLD/NSW, WA, SA), coordinated by a purpose-built authority with 15-year mandate, funded tri-laterally (federal + state royalties + employer levy), CFMEU as governance partner. Manages workers through four-stage pipeline (audit → gap employment → modular training → placement) tracked via digital skills passport. Must be robust to commodity volatility, policy reversal, automation, trainer scarcity, and free-riding. Training quality as export competitiveness asset.
- **Key shifts:** (1) "One workforce" → three distinct corridors with different transfer ratios; (2) TAFE updates insufficient → university partnerships required for processing chemistry; (3) No rational $160K coal miner transitions voluntarily → economic bridges essential; (4) Timeline collision demands active gap-period management; (5) 30-40 qualified trainers is the binding constraint; (6) Training quality = trade advantage via supply chain certification.

### Perspective Walk

#### Perspective 1: The Water Chemist's View
- **What it revealed:** Skill translation is asymmetric. Coal miners already handle water chemistry (acid mine drainage, tailings, dust suppression) but frame it as waste management. In lithium processing, identical chemical intuitions (pH control, precipitation, filtration) become core value-creation. Retraining for 8-12% of workforce (CHPP operators, environmental officers, tailings technicians) is a 6-week cognitive reframe, not 12-month education. For the remaining 88%, the gap is real. Creates a two-speed pipeline the single module structure doesn't accommodate.
- **New angle suggested:** What do the other 88% (haul truck drivers, shotfirers, longwall operators) actually carry?

#### Perspective 2: The Underground Body
- **What it revealed:** Underground competency is the scarcest skill in Australian critical minerals, and coal has the largest reservoir. Hard-rock lithium (Greenbushes, Finniss) and rare earth extraction (Nolans, Browns Range) are going underground. Need 3,000-5,000 underground miners by 2032. Framework should segment by underground vs. surface experience, not geography — this cuts more usefully across all corridors. Underground coal miners (Hunter Valley, Illawarra, Central QLD) are more valuable to critical minerals than geographically-closer iron ore workers. Mobility support is the mechanism that unlocks the highest-value transfer.
- **New angle suggested:** What happens to receiving communities when you pipe in 500 ex-coal families?

#### Perspective 3: The Receiving Town
- **What it revealed:** Municipal infrastructure constraint sits outside the training system. Ravensthorpe (pop. ~500) can't house 500 families. Tennant Creek health system can't absorb a surge. Kalgoorlie absorbs FIFO only, hollowing origin towns. Gap-period employment inadvertently delays destination infrastructure buildout. Same chicken-and-egg that killed every prior Australian regional development scheme (Ord River, Pilbara Cities). Only breaker: a state development corporation with capital allocation authority and housing mandate.
- **New angle suggested:** Who pays? The money question hasn't been asked.

#### Perspective 4: The Rent Curve
- **What it revealed:** Fiscal transition is temporally mismatched. Coal royalties high NOW but declining (QLD $6.2B FY23 peak, NSW $1.8B). Critical mineral royalties low now but growing. 5-8 year crossover period (2027-2035) where total royalties dip — exactly when retraining investment peaks. Solution: Transition Bonds securitising future critical mineral royalties, front-loading investment. But coal royalties go to QLD/NSW, mineral royalties to WA/SA/NT. Need a federal fiscal transfer mechanism — politically radioactive but mathematically necessary.
- **New angle suggested:** Royalty geography mismatch is a governance problem. What coalitions make this possible?

#### Perspective 5: The Union Hall at Midnight
- **What it revealed:** Trust deficit determines participation. Every prior transition ("retraining" = "redundancy + short course"). Hazelwood reference trauma: ~15-20% participation. Three features needed for 60-70%: (1) wage maintenance during training ($120-180K/year, not stipend), (2) guaranteed employment from named company at named site, (3) union co-governance of training system. Gap between 20% and 70% participation is the difference between managed transition and regional catastrophe. Trust architecture is the real constraint.
- **New angle suggested:** Union co-governance requires union technical capacity in critical minerals.

#### Perspective 6: The Trainer's Trainer
- **What it revealed:** The 200-300 trainer shortage is actually a 30-40 person catastrophe at the critical minerals-specific layer. Solution: reverse apprenticeship — bring 15-20 operational staff from Greenbushes/Mt Weld/Kwinana into 6-month trainer development. Tiny intervention (~$3-5M) that unblocks entire pipeline. Cheapest, highest-leverage investment in the entire transition. But requires employers to cooperate in creating their own labour competition.
- **New angle suggested:** Employers building a public good competitors will poach — classic collective action problem.

#### Perspective 7: The Minerals Council Boardroom
- **What it revealed:** Australia already ran this experiment — Mining Industry Skills Centre (MISC), mid-2000s to early-2010s. Collapsed because: (a) boom = companies poach not train, (b) bust = companies cut levy, (c) governance captured by large miners. Fix: counter-cyclical levies (higher during booms) + portable training allocations (attached to worker via skills passport, not employer). Skills passport is not just a credential record — it's an anti-poaching mechanism making investment recoverable through market transparency.
- **New angle suggested:** Skills passport increases mobility but receiving towns need stability. Tension.

#### Perspective 8: The First Nations Land Council
- **What it revealed:** Most critical mineral deposits on Native Title land. ILUAs include 15-30% local Indigenous employment targets (Nolans ~400 jobs = 60-120 Indigenous positions). Mainstream retraining framework culturally inappropriate for this cohort. Need a dual-stream system: one for transitioning miners, one for Indigenous workforce development with different entry points, pedagogies (on-country learning, flexible scheduling), timelines, and metrics. More critically: Indigenous social licence is a prerequisite for projects proceeding — without community support, no projects, no jobs, no destination for retrained workers.
- **New angle suggested:** International regulatory dimension — EU/US due diligence requirements.

#### Perspective 9: The Brussels Compliance Officer
- **What it revealed:** Retraining system is a trade compliance asset. EU Critical Raw Materials Act + US IRA "friendly nation" provisions require workforce standard documentation. Skills passport becomes an export supply chain document. Australian lithium with full workforce provenance could command 3-8% premium ($500-1,500/tonne) over Chinese-processed material in ESG-sensitive markets. Retraining system pays for itself through trade premiums if designed with export certification from day one.
- **New angle suggested:** Reinforcing oscillation insight (supply chain certification). Approaching convergence.

#### Perspective 10: The Commodity Trader's Spreadsheet
- **What it revealed:** Project cancellations during price downturns (Albemarle paused Kemerton Train 3, Liontown delayed Kathleen Valley) wipe out destination jobs. Training workers for evaporated jobs is worse than no training. Need a demand-side circuit breaker — slows training intake when pipelines contract. But this contradicts guaranteed employment (Perspective 5). Resolution: government employer-of-last-resort during commodity downturns — gap-period employment reframed as insurance mechanism, not just sequencing tool.
- **New angle suggested:** Refining existing structure. Converging.

### Walk Summary
- **Perspectives taken:** 10 genuine, distinct perspectives
- **Key revelations:** (1) Two-speed pipeline + underground skill premium; (2) 30-person trainer catastrophe solvable with $3-5M reverse apprenticeship; (3) Trust architecture (wage maintenance + guaranteed employment + union co-governance) determines 20% vs 70% participation; (4) Indigenous dual-stream is prerequisite not parallel consideration; (5) Transition Bonds securitising future royalties solve fiscal trough
- **Seed types used:** None — each perspective emerged from the previous

### Convergence Signal
- **Status:** Converging
- **Evidence:** Last two perspectives refined existing structure rather than opening new territory. Major structural elements form a coherent, cross-referencing system.
- **Recommendation:** Hand off to ConvergeAndEvaluate. Key design tension to resolve: worker mobility vs. community stability vs. demand volatility, with trust architecture as the binding constraint.

### Candidate Position

The retraining architecture is a governance-wrapped, counter-cyclically-funded, supply-constrained, spatially-matched training pipeline with an export compliance premium — a national transition infrastructure that solves in order: (1) institutional trust through union co-governance, Indigenous dual-stream, and portable credentials; (2) fiscal timing through securitised future royalties and employer-of-last-resort insurance; (3) trainer scarcity through reverse apprenticeship of 15-20 operational staff; (4) spatial matching through underground/surface segmentation and receiving-community capacity assessment; with training quality doubling as an ESG export asset.

### Evaluative Interrogation

#### Question Selection Rationale

**Selected:** Category 3 (Integrity Checks) + Category 2 (Primitive Assessment) + Category 4 (Predictive Power)

Why these three, and why NOT the other two:

- **Category 3 (Integrity Checks)** is essential because the candidate was built from 10 perspectives that each shifted the boundary. The oscillation expanded the problem from "retraining" to "sociotechnical transition system" — has the process drifted from what's actually needed?
- **Category 2 (Primitive Assessment)** because the candidate recombines a lot of primitives (skills passports, levies, bonds, authorities, dual-streams) — are the building blocks right? Is anything missing?
- **Category 4 (Predictive Power)** because the candidate claims to be an architecture — if it's real, it should predict things we haven't explicitly identified yet.
- **Category 1 (Generative Potential)** skipped — fractal potential already confirmed in convergence detection. Re-testing would be redundant.
- **Category 5 (Simplicity Test)** skipped — the one-sentence synthesis already passed during convergence.

#### Category 3: Integrity Checks

**Q: Does this serve the larger intent, or has the process drifted?**

The original question was "What should the retraining architecture look like?" The candidate answers a bigger question: "What should the workforce transition system look like?" This IS drift — but it's productive drift. However, the candidate risks overwhelming implementation by solving everything at once. A worker in Muswellbrook doesn't need a national authority and Transition Bonds before they can start a lithium processing module. The architecture should be decomposable into a minimum viable transition (what works without the full system) and the full system (what makes it work at scale and duration). The candidate lacks this decomposition.

**Implication:** Needs phased implementation path — what you do in year 1 with 3 trainers and no national authority yet. MODIFICATION needed.

**Q: Would this survive contact with messy reality?**

Three reality tests:

(1) **Political reality:** A federal fiscal transfer from WA mineral royalties to NSW coal transition? WA will resist. The Murray-Darling Basin Authority analogy is apt — it took a near-crisis to force cooperation. The critical minerals transition doesn't yet have an equivalent forcing function. **Partial pass.**

(2) **Bureaucratic reality:** A new national authority requires legislation, intergovernmental agreements, and a 2-3 year establishment period. During that period, coal closures continue and workers have no system. **Fail — gap between now and institutional readiness is unaddressed.**

(3) **Individual worker reality:** A 40-year-old shotfirer in Singleton making $165K is told about a chain of dependencies too long and too abstract. The candidate needs a concrete first step visible to that individual: a specific company, a specific site, a specific start date, a specific wage. **Needs "first 100 workers" concrete pilot.**

**Q: Is this just cleverness, or does it have weight?**

It has weight. Each cluster is grounded in a real institutional failure or constraint, not in theoretical reasoning. Hazelwood failure, MISC collapse, Jabiluka shutdown. **PASS.**

#### Category 2: Primitive Assessment

**Q: Are the existing primitives sufficient, or is something MISSING?**

Three missing primitives identified:

1. **Labour market matching engine** — The skills passport is a credential record, but the system also needs an active marketplace connecting completing trainees with approved project positions. Not passive transparency; active matching.

2. **Psychosocial support infrastructure** — Identity disruption mentioned in passing (Cycle 4) but never developed structurally. Latrobe Valley evidence shows technical retraining without psychosocial support produces high attrition, substance abuse spikes, and family breakdown. Peer mentoring network embedded in corridor hubs is the mechanism.

3. **Automation-readiness as core curriculum** — Critical minerals operations are 30-40% automated from day one. Training workers for manual-era roles creates a second obsolescence within 5 years. Autonomous systems supervision, remote operations, and data analytics must be cross-cutting requirements, not elective.

**Q: Do any primitives need TRANSFORMATION?**

Two transformations:

1. **Gap-period employment → insurance product.** Structure as mutual obligation (worker/employer/government contributions) for political durability, not government spending program.

2. **Skills passport → multi-function coordination backbone.** One platform tracking skills audit, gap analysis, training progress, placement status, wage bridge entitlement, and post-placement outcomes. Not three separate systems stitched together.

#### Category 4: Predictive Power

**Q: Does this candidate predict things we haven't seen yet?**

Three predictions:

1. **Trainer pipeline triggers first political crisis.** If the $3-5M reverse apprenticeship intervention is not funded BEFORE the national program launches, the program will fail publicly within 18 months. Testable prediction with clear timeline.

2. **WA will resist fiscal transfer, creating a federalism crisis.** WA generates ~65% of critical mineral value but QLD/NSW bear disproportionate cost. Same dynamic that stalled the MRRT. Architecture needs a WA-acceptable alternative.

3. **Indigenous dual-stream will outperform mainstream on retention.** Indigenous-led, on-country training with community-embedded employment will produce 75%+ 5-year retention vs. 55-65% mainstream. Because retention is a community problem, not a skill problem.

**Q: Does it suggest a missing element that should exist but doesn't?**

Yes: **Adaptive control function.** The system has no feedback loop — no mechanism by which outcomes flow back to modify the training pipeline in real time. Who decides to slow intake when lithium prices crash? Who accelerates downstream manufacturing module when a battery plant is announced? The architecture needs a "thermostat" — real-time monitoring reading skills passport data, project pipeline data, and commodity data to modulate training throughput. Without it, the architecture is open-loop.

### Verdict: MODIFY

The candidate has genuine structural merit — high convergence confidence, fractal potential, predictive power, and grounding in real institutional failures. But it needs five modifications:

1. **Add phased implementation:** Decompose into minimum viable transition → pilot scale (first 100 workers) → corridor scale → national scale.
2. **Add three missing primitives:** Labour market matching engine, psychosocial support infrastructure, automation-readiness as core curriculum.
3. **Add adaptive control function:** Real-time feedback loop modulating training throughput based on project pipeline, commodity prices, and placement outcomes.
4. **Transform gap employment into insurance product:** Mutual obligation structure for political durability.
5. **Condition the export compliance premium:** Design architecture to function WITHOUT the ESG premium; treat as upside.

**What does NOT need modification:** Four-cluster structural architecture, digital skills passport as coordination backbone, three-corridor federated model, reverse apprenticeship trainer intervention, Indigenous dual-stream.
