# Tier 3 D-Test

## Question

Does the composition `Dual-Speed Governance × Explicit State Machine Backbone × Composable Plugin Architecture` (optionally `+ Bounded Buffer With Overflow Policy`) uniquely predict `continuity-preserving self-amendment under pressure`, and is that prediction unavailable from any individual motif or pair alone?

## Candidate Emergent Property

### Five-part chain

1. `Notice` — detect that current structure is no longer viable
2. `Represent` — refer to an explicit model of that structure
3. `Authorize` — govern whether structural change is allowed
4. `Install` — recompose through modular substitution
5. `Continue` — maintain operational identity through the change

The d-test passes only if there is a clean capability gap between individuals/pairs and the triple/quad.

## D — Systematic Capability Test

### Domain-grounded observations

- Constitutional emergency systems are strong on `Notice`, `Represent`, and `Authorize`; they are weaker on true `Install`, because they often frame change as suspension or exceptional interpretation rather than modular recomposition.
- Immune remodelling is the strongest natural full-chain case: sensing, antigen presentation, tolerance/co-stimulation gates, clonal/effector installation, and memory-preserving continuation are all explicit.
- Live software reconfiguration is the strongest engineered case for `Install` and `Continue`, but often relies on runtime policy or environment assumptions for `Authorize`.
- Across all three domains, `pressure` matters: emergency, pathogen load, or runtime change drive the need for self-amendment.
- No domain is cleanly explained by one Tier 2 motif alone; all require composite capability.

### Pair and triple interpretation

- `DSG` alone authorizes but cannot represent or install.
- `ESMB` alone represents but cannot authorize or install.
- `CPA` alone installs but cannot notice, represent, or authorize.
- `BBWOP` alone notices pressure and can preserve continuation boundaries, but cannot represent or authorize structural change.
- Every pair misses at least one of the three middle steps: `Represent`, `Authorize`, `Install`.
- The triple `DSG × ESMB × CPA` is the first level that can complete the full five-part chain.
- The quad adds pressure robustness and salience. It does not replace the triple's core capability, but it makes the chain more likely to trigger under real operating stress.

## I — Cross-domain Interpretation

### Constitutional law

- Best fit: `DSG × ESMB × CPA × BBWOP`, but asymmetrically.
- `DSG` -> constitutional vs emergency path
- `ESMB` -> explicit state/exception model of order, entrenchment, legality
- `CPA` -> amendment / delegated powers / interpretive substitution as limited install analogue
- `BBWOP` -> emergency threshold and exceptional-pressure condition
- Caveat: `Install` is weakest here because constitutional change is not naturally modular.

### Immune escalation

- Best fit: full quad.
- `BBWOP` matters materially here: self/non-self thresholds, tissue boundaries, inflammatory load, and tolerance gates are not optional.
- The domain supports the five-part chain most fully.

### Live software reconfiguration

- Best fit: triple for dev-time systems, quad for production-grade continuity under pressure.
- `DSG × ESMB × CPA` is enough for many hot-reload and module-swap patterns.
- `+BBWOP` becomes important when uptime, rollback envelope, isolation boundary, or fault containment become real.

### What the three domains jointly show

- The triple explains the core self-amendment loop.
- The quad explains self-amendment under stress more robustly.
- The gap is not between “single motif” and “all four.” It is specifically between pairs and the triple.

## R — Verdict

### d partially passes

The strongest honest verdict is `d partially passes`.

### Why not full pass?

- At the abstract capability level, there is a clean gap: no pair can complete `Represent -> Authorize -> Install`; only the triple and quad can.
- But the domain evidence is still uneven:
  - constitutional law supports the chain conceptually, but `Install` remains weak/indirect
  - immune remodelling strongly supports the full chain, but current evidence is partly conceptual rather than directly scraped from ideal sources
  - software reconfiguration strongly supports `Install` and `Continue`, but sometimes compresses `Represent` and `Authorize` into thinner runtime checks
- So the emergent capability looks real, but the empirical boundary is not yet clean enough for an earned Tier 3 promotion.

### What is earned now

- The triple `DSG × ESMB × CPA` is the minimal composition that appears capable of continuity-preserving self-amendment.
- The quad `+BBWOP` is the stronger under-pressure form.
- This supports the compositional recurse hypothesis and sharpens the Tier 3 candidate family.

### What would upgrade this to full pass?

- 2-3 tightly worked case studies per domain where all five links are independently identifiable and temporally ordered.
- Stronger evidence that `Represent`, `Authorize`, and `Install` are mechanistically distinct rather than analyst-imposed splits.
- One cross-domain comparison showing the same failure mode when any one of the three middle links is missing.

### Falsification conditions

- If a pair can be shown to complete all five steps in a serious domain without smuggling in the missing third capability, `d` fails.
- If the five-part chain only works after analyst-imposed abstraction and not in domain-native descriptions, `d` weakens.
- If pressure (`BBWOP`) proves necessary in all credible cases, then the minimal triple claim is too strong and must be revised upward to the quad.

## Recommendation

- Do not recommend Tier 3 promotion yet.
- Treat this as a sharpened near-pass:
  - minimal candidate stack: `DSG × ESMB × CPA`
  - stress-robust candidate stack: `DSG × ESMB × CPA × BBWOP`
- The next move is exactly the one already implied by the afternoon handoff: design a stronger `d` test around an emergent property that is impossible with any pair and unmistakable in at least two unrelated domains.
