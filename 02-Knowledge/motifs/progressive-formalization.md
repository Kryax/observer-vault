# Progressive Formalization

## Structural Description

Material passes through stages of increasing structural order, with each stage preserving essential content while adding constraint. The progression is not loss — the original insight survives at every level of formalization. What changes is the precision of its boundaries, the rigour of its relationships, and its accessibility to systematic operations (search, composition, verification). Amorphous becomes crystalline, but the crystal remembers the liquid it came from.

## Domain-Independent Formulation

## Falsification Conditions

- If formalization loses essential content rather than preserving it (i.e., the crystal no longer remembers the liquid), the pattern breaks — this would be "lossy compression", not progressive formalization
- If the stages are not genuinely progressive (i.e., no increase in constraint or operational accessibility between stages), this is just "rewriting in different formats"
- If the progression happens in a single discontinuous step rather than through stages, it's a phase transition, not progressive formalization
