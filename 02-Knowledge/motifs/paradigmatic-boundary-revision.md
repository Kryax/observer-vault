# Paradigmatic Boundary Revision

## Structural Description

A third-order distinction operation: not creating boundaries, not shifting boundaries, but revising the system of categorisation that determines what counts as a valid boundary. The meta-framework for distinction-making itself gets restructured. This is paradigm shift at the level of the distinction apparatus, not at the level of what is distinguished.

## Domain-Independent Formulation

Third-order distinction: revision of the categorisation system that determines what counts as a valid boundary, not the boundaries themselves.

## Instances

### Instance 1: Philosophy of Science
- **Domain:** Philosophy of Science (Kuhn)
- **Expression:** Paradigm shifts restructure not just which theories are accepted but what counts as a valid explanation, what counts as evidence, what questions are legitimate.

### Instance 2: Mathematical Logic
- **Domain:** Foundations of Mathematics
- **Expression:** Revisions of logical frameworks — the shift from classical to intuitionistic logic changes what counts as a valid proof, not just which proofs are accepted.
- **Discovery date:** 2026-03-22

### Instance 3: Biology / Taxonomy
- **Domain:** Biological Classification
- **Expression:** The phenetic-to-cladistic shift changed what counts as a valid classification axis (observable similarity vs evolutionary descent), not just how organisms are classified.
- **Discovery date:** 2026-03-22
