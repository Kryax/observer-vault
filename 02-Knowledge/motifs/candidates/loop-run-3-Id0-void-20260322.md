## The d0 test

A candidate instance is genuinely d0 (not d1+) if and only if:

1. It describes a **structural arrangement** in which parts are connected, not a process by which they become connected
2. Integration is a **positional fact** — it holds at a snapshot, not over a trajectory
3. Removing the coupling does not reveal a process that was occurring; it reveals a structural property that was present
4. The pattern can be stated without reference to time, change, or dynamics — it is synchronic, not diachronic

The critical distinction: d1 is a process of integrating (convergence, coupling-in-progress). d0 is the structural fact of being integrated. d1 asks "how does integration happen?" d0 asks "what does it look like when a system IS integrated?"

## Candidate: Structural Coupling as Ground State

**Working name**: Structural Coupling as Ground State

**Domain-independent description**: A system in which constituent parts stand in relations of mutual determination such that the properties of the whole are not decomposable into independent properties of the parts. Integration is not something the system does — it is something the system IS. The coupling is a structural fact, observable at any frozen instant, not a process that unfolds over time. The whole constrains the parts and the parts constrain the whole simultaneously, as a static configuration. This is the zeroth-order ground state of the Integrate axis: the positional fact that integration obtains.

### Instance 1: Quantum Physics — Nonseparability and Entanglement

- **Domain**: Physics (Quantum Mechanics)
- **Structural property**: In quantum mechanics, an entangled state is one where the quantum state of the compound system cannot be expressed as a product of the states of its subsystems. This is nonseparability: the whole has properties (correlations, joint probability distributions) that are not determined by the properties of the parts taken independently. Crucially, nonseparability is a structural property of the state vector at a given moment — it is not a process. The entangled state simply IS nonseparable. It does not become nonseparable over time (the creation of entanglement is a d1 process; the entangled state itself, once it obtains, is d0). Einstein's "separability principle" was precisely the claim that spatially separated systems should have independent real states; quantum mechanics violates this as a positional fact, not as a dynamic.
- **Derivative order justification**: d0 = the compound system IS in an entangled state; the whole has properties the parts lack. This is a snapshot property — at any instant, the joint state is nonseparable. d1 would be the process of entanglement creation or decoherence. **The nonseparable state itself is d0: a static structural fact of integration.**
- **Source**: `ocp:sep/physics-holism` (trust: 0.585)
- **Key references**: Schrodinger (1935) "Discussion of Probability Relations Between Separated Systems"; Healey (1991) "Holism and Nonseparability"; Howard (1985) "Einstein on Locality and Separability"; Teller (1986) "Relational Holism and Quantum Mechanics"; Bohm (1980) *Wholeness and the Implicate Order*; SEP "Holism and Nonseparability in Physics"

### Instance 2: Mereology — Composition as Structural Fact

- **Domain**: Metaphysics / Formal Ontology
- **Structural property**: Mereology studies the part-whole relation as a timeless structural relation. When parts compose a whole, the composition relation holds as a positional fact. Classical mereology (Lesniewski, Goodman, Leonard) defines composition, overlap, and parthood as static formal relations. The composition question ("under what conditions do objects compose a further object?") is not about a process of composing but about when a structural arrangement obtains. Van Inwagen's Special Composition Question asks when composition occurs — the answer describes a structural condition, not a temporal process. Universalism (composition always occurs) makes integration maximally ground-state: any collection of entities automatically IS a whole. Even restricted composition views (composition only when parts form a life, or are fastened together, etc.) define integration as a structural configuration that either obtains or does not.
- **Derivative order justification**: d0 = composition IS a structural fact — parts either compose a whole or they do not, at a given instant. The relation is synchronic. d1 would be assembly, construction, or aggregation — processes of coming-to-compose. **Mereological composition as studied formally is d0: the static structural fact that parts are integrated into a whole.**
- **Source**: `ocp:sep/mereology` (trust: 0.634), `ocp:sep/ordinary-objects` (trust: 0.680)
- **Key references**: Lesniewski (1916) *Foundations of the General Theory of Sets*; Goodman (1951) *The Structure of Appearance*; Lewis (1991) *Parts of Classes*; van Inwagen (1990) *Material Beings*; Simons (1987) *Parts: A Study in Ontology*; SEP "Mereology"

### Instance 3: Epistemology — Coherence as Static Structural Relation

- **Domain**: Epistemology / Philosophy of Truth
- **Structural property**: The coherence theory of truth defines truth as a structural relation among propositions: a proposition is true if and only if it coheres with a specified set of propositions. Coherence here is not a process of making-coherent; it is a structural property that a set of propositions either has or lacks at any given moment. The coherence relation — whether understood as consistency, mutual entailment, or mutual explanatory support (Bradley 1914) — is synchronic. A belief system IS coherent as a structural fact. The "web of belief" (Quine, Davidson) is a spatial metaphor for a static structural arrangement of mutual support relations. Legal coherence (Dworkin's "integrity") similarly describes a normative system as being in a state of internal consistency — the law IS coherent, as a structural property, not as a process of becoming coherent.
- **Derivative order justification**: d0 = a set of propositions IS coherent — the mutual support relations hold at a snapshot. d1 would be the process of achieving coherence (belief revision, reflective equilibrium as a dynamic). **Coherence as a structural property of a belief system is d0: the positional fact that the elements support each other.**
- **Source**: `ocp:sep/truth-coherence` (trust: 0.507), `ocp:sep/legal-reas-interpret` (trust: 0.504)
- **Key references**: Bradley (1914) *Essays on Truth and Reality*; Davidson (1986) "A Coherence Theory of Truth and Knowledge"; Rescher (1973) *The Coherence Theory of Truth*; BonJour (1985) *The Structure of Empirical Knowledge*; Dworkin (1986) *Law's Empire* (integrity as coherence); MacCormick (1984) "Coherence in Legal Justification"; SEP "The Coherence Theory of Truth", "Interpretation and Coherence in Legal Reasoning"

### Instance 4: Social Science — Holistic Social Structure as Irreducible Whole

- **Domain**: Social Science / Sociology
- **Structural property**: Methodological holism holds that social structures (institutions, cultures, norms) exist as wholes whose properties are not reducible to properties of individual members. A social structure IS an integrated arrangement — the institution exists as a structural fact, not as a process of instituting. Durkheim's "social facts" are external to and constraining upon individuals — they are structural properties of the collective that exist at any given moment. The density of a social network, the cohesion of a group, the normative structure of an institution — these are synchronic, snapshot-describable properties. They are not processes of integrating but states of being integrated. Supervenience without reducibility: the social whole has properties that covary with but are not identical to the properties of its individual parts.
- **Derivative order justification**: d0 = a social structure IS an integrated whole. The institution exists, the network has a density, the culture has a coherent set of norms — all at a given instant. d1 would be institution-building, cultural change, norm formation. **Social structure as structural fact is d0: the positional property that the social whole is integrated.**
- **Source**: `ocp:sep/holism-social` (trust: 0.485), `ocp:sep/supervenience` (trust: 0.617)
- **Key references**: Durkheim (1895/1938) *The Rules of Sociological Method*; Mandelbaum (1955) "Societal Facts"; Jackson & Pettit (1992) "Structural Explanation in Social Theory"; Elder-Vass (2010) *The Causal Power of Social Structures*; Zahle (2007) "Holism and Supervenience"; Sawyer (2005) *Social Emergence*; SEP "Methodological Holism in the Social Sciences"

### Instance 5: Linguistics — Meaning Holism as Structural Interdependence

- **Domain**: Linguistics / Philosophy of Language
- **Structural property**: Meaning holism holds that the meanings of all words in a language are interdependent — the meaning of any word is determined by its relations to all other words. This is a structural property of the semantic network: at any moment, the meaning of "kill" depends on its relations to "cause", "die", "living", and so on through the entire language. The interdependence is not a process of meanings becoming connected; it is a structural fact about how meanings ARE connected. Quine's "web of belief" and Sellars' "inferential role semantics" describe meaning as constituted by the totality of inferential connections — a static structural configuration. The "unit of empirical significance is the whole of science" (Quine 1951) states a structural fact about the semantic network, not a process of integration.
- **Derivative order justification**: d0 = the semantic network IS holistically structured — every term's meaning is constituted by its relations to all others. This is a snapshot property. d1 would be semantic change, language evolution, or meaning shift. **Semantic holism as structural fact is d0: the positional property that meanings are integrated.**
- **Source**: `ocp:sep/meaning-holism` (trust: 0.501)
- **Key references**: Quine (1951) "Two Dogmas of Empiricism"; Sellars (1974) "Meaning as Functional Classification"; Davidson (1984) *Inquiries into Truth and Interpretation*; Fodor & Lepore (1992) *Holism: A Shoppers Guide*; Block (1986) "Advertisement for a Semantics for Psychology"; Brandom (1994) *Making it Explicit*; SEP "Meaning Holism"

### Instance 6: Graph Theory / Software Architecture — Structural Connectivity

- **Domain**: Computer Science / Systems Engineering
- **Structural property**: A connected graph is one in which every pair of vertices has a path between them. Connectivity is a structural property of the graph at a given instant — it is not a process of connecting but a fact about the topology. In software architecture, coupling describes the degree to which modules depend on each other. A tightly coupled system IS integrated as a structural fact: at any snapshot, module A depends on module B depends on module C. The dependency graph is a static object. Measures like cyclomatic complexity, coupling coefficients, and cohesion metrics all describe structural properties of the system at a point in time. Conway's Law ("organizations design systems that mirror their own communication structure") is a structural isomorphism claim — the software architecture IS coupled to the organizational structure, as a positional fact.
- **Derivative order justification**: d0 = a system IS connected/coupled as a graph-theoretic property at a snapshot. d1 would be the process of adding edges, refactoring dependencies, or building connections. **Structural connectivity as a graph property is d0: the positional fact that nodes are integrated.**

- **Key references**: Bollobas (1998) *Modern Graph Theory*; Diestel (2017) *Graph Theory*; Conway (1968) "How Do Committees Invent?"; Parnas (1972) "On the Criteria to be Used in Decomposing Systems into Modules"; Bass, Clements & Kazman (2012) *Software Architecture in Practice*; Node.js Best Practices (structural dependency management); Domain-Driven Hexagon (coupling/cohesion as structural design properties)
