## New Domain Instances

### Instance 7: Developmental Biology — Evo-Devo Constraint Coupling
- **Domain:** Developmental Biology / Evo-Devo
- **Expression:** Developmental constraints are structural couplings between organism and environment that exist prior to any adaptive evolution. The embryonic body plan constrains what evolution can produce — not because of selection, but because the developmental machinery creates mutual constraints between tissue types, signalling pathways, and morphogenetic fields. Hox gene clusters are structurally coupled to body segment identity: the coupling is the ground state on which adaptive modification operates. No agent designed the coupling; it emerged from physical adjacency and shared regulatory machinery.

  The evo-devo insight is that constraint coupling (not just natural selection) shapes evolutionary possibility. Developmental bias — the tendency for mutations to produce certain phenotypes more readily than others — is structural coupling at the molecular level shaping macroevolutionary outcomes.
- **Discovery date:** 2026-03-23
- **Source:** bottom-up (`ocp:sep/evolution-development` — SEP entry covers developmental constraints, modularity, and evolvability)
- **Structural fit:** Strong. The coupling is passive (not designed), arises from shared interface (regulatory networks), and constrains higher-order processes (evolution). Precisely the ground-state role described by the motif.

### Instance 8: Ecology — Mutualism as Structural Coupling
- **Domain:** Ecology / Community Ecology
- **Expression:** Mycorrhizal networks structurally couple plant root systems to fungal hyphae. The coupling arises from physical contact and chemical exchange at the root-fungus interface. Neither organism "decides" to couple; the coupling emerges from shared soil substrate and compatible biochemistry. Once coupled, the plant constrains the fungus (providing photosynthate) and the fungus constrains the plant (providing mineral nutrients). The coupling is the ground state of the mutualism — all higher-order ecological relationships (nutrient cycling, community structure, forest resilience) build on top of it.

  Coral-zooxanthellae symbiosis follows the same pattern: the coupling arises from shared tissue interface, is passive (no agent integrates), and constrains both partners.
- **Discovery date:** 2026-03-23
- **Source:** domain knowledge (grounded in ecological theory; adjacent to `ocp:sep/systems-synthetic-biology`)
- **Structural fit:** Strong. The key structural test: is the coupling passive (ground state) or active (intentional integration)? Mycorrhizal coupling passes — the physical contact is the coupling, not any deliberate exchange protocol.

### Instance 9: Economics — Market Coupling (Walrasian Interconnection)
- **Domain:** Economics / Market Theory
- **Expression:** In general equilibrium theory, markets for different goods become structurally coupled through the budget constraint: any change in one market's price affects demand in all other markets because consumer budgets are shared. This coupling is not designed — it emerges from the fact that buyers have finite budgets and must allocate across goods. The coupling IS the budget constraint; no agent performs the integration.

  Walras's Law (the sum of excess demands across all markets is zero) is a formal expression of this structural coupling: markets cannot be independent because the budget constraint links them. International trade creates structural coupling between national economies through shared currency markets, supply chains, and capital flows — the coupling emerges from interface contact, not from trade agreements (those are the intentional overlay).
- **Discovery date:** 2026-03-23
- **Source:** domain knowledge (grounded in economic theory)
- **Structural fit:** Strong. The budget constraint is the passive coupling; trade agreements and tariffs are the intentional integration built on top. The ground-state claim holds: all higher-order economic integration (trade deals, currency unions, fiscal coordination) operates on top of the passive coupling created by shared budget constraints.

### Instance 10: Neuroscience — Hebbian Coupling
- **Domain:** Neuroscience / Neural Network Theory
- **Expression:** "Neurons that fire together wire together" (Hebb's rule). Neural populations that are simultaneously active become structurally coupled through synaptic strengthening. The coupling is passive — no agent decides which neurons to couple. The shared interface is temporal co-activation. Once coupled, the populations mutually constrain each other: activating one tends to activate the other. This is the ground state on which all higher-order neural integration (feature binding, working memory, cortical maps) is built.

  Spike-timing-dependent plasticity (STDP) is the biophysical mechanism: the coupling is determined by millisecond-scale temporal proximity at the synapse. The coupling is genuinely passive — it requires only co-occurrence, not any higher-order process.
- **Discovery date:** 2026-03-23
- **Source:** domain knowledge (grounded in computational neuroscience)
- **Structural fit:** Strong. Hebbian coupling is arguably the canonical instance of passive structural coupling in a physical system. The coupling (synaptic strengthening) arises from shared interface (temporal co-activation), is passive (no supervisory signal needed), and creates the ground state on which higher-order integration operates.

## Structural Sharpening

The alien domains reveal a structural invariant more precisely:

**Core invariant:** When two systems share an interface over time, mutual constraint emerges without any agent performing integration. The constraint IS the coupling; the coupling IS the relationship.

**Discriminating test:** Remove the shared interface → the coupling dissolves. If the constraint persists without interface contact, it's not structural coupling but something else (e.g., hardcoded dependency, designed integration).

**Non-instances to clarify the boundary:**
- **Designed APIs** are NOT structural coupling — they are intentional integration. The coupling is planned, not emergent.
- **Gravitational interaction** is structural coupling at the physical level but may be too fundamental to be useful as a motif instance (everything couples gravitationally).
- **Hyrum's Law** (Instance 5) is the boundary case: the coupling is unintentional but arises from observation rather than physical interface. Keeping it as a valid instance — the "interface" is the observable API surface, and the coupling arises from consumer dependency.
