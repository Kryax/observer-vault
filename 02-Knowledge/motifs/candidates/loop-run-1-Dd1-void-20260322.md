## Candidate: Boundary Drift

**Working name**: Boundary Drift

**Domain-independent description**: A pattern in which an existing system of distinctions undergoes directed, incremental displacement over time — boundaries between categories shift, borderline cases migrate from one side to another, and the classification landscape has a measurable trajectory — without the classification framework itself being replaced (that would be d2/d3).

### Instance 1: Philosophy / Epistemology — Conceptual Change and Theory Revision

- **Domain**: Philosophy of Science / Epistemology
- **Process**: The pessimistic meta-induction (Laudan 1981, surveyed in SEP "Realism and Theory Change") documents how scientific categories drift incrementally under empirical pressure. Caloric becomes heat-as-energy; the boundary between "planet" and "non-planet" shifts (Pluto's reclassification). The realist response (Psillos 1999, Poincare 1902) identifies that what persists through theory change is relational structure — but the *boundaries* of what counts as a legitimate theoretical entity drift steadily. This is not paradigm shift (d3, which changes the meta-criteria for valid distinctions) but the directed, first-order movement of where existing category boundaries fall. Belief revision theory (AGM model, SEP "Logic of Belief Revision") formalises this: contraction removes a distinction, expansion adds one, revision shifts the boundary of what is believed — and iterated revision traces a trajectory through belief space.
- **Derivative order justification**: d1 because the distinctions themselves are changing position over time — the category boundary is moving. The classification framework (what counts as a valid category) remains fixed (that's d0). The mechanism that drives boundary change (selective pressure, new evidence) would be d2. D/d1 captures the velocity: the rate and direction of boundary displacement.
- **Source**: `ocp:sep/realism-theory-change` (trust: 0.497), `ocp:sep/logic-belief-revision` (trust: 0.438), `ocp:sep/change` (trust: 0.527)
- **Key references**: Laudan (1981) "A Confutation of Convergent Realism"; Psillos (1999) *Scientific Realism: How Science Tracks Truth*; Alchourron, Gardenfors & Makinson (1985) AGM belief revision; Poincare (1902) *La Science et l'Hypothese*

### Instance 2: Philosophy / Metaphysics — Vagueness and Sorites Boundary Dynamics

- **Domain**: Philosophy of Language / Metaphysics
- **Process**: Vague predicates (SEP "Vagueness", "Sorites Paradox") exhibit boundary drift as a core structural feature. The boundary between "heap" and "not-heap", "bald" and "not-bald", shifts under contextual pressure. Graff (2000) formalises this as "interest-relative" vagueness: the boundary between categories shifts based on the practical interests of the classifier. Raffman (1994, 2014) shows that competent speakers systematically shift category boundaries during forced-march sorites — the boundary drifts through the series as each judgment creates context for the next. Higher-order vagueness (SEP "Vagueness" section on higher-order) shows that even the boundary *of* the boundary drifts (borderline cases of borderline cases). Contextualist theories (Shapiro 2006, Kamp 1981) model this as category boundaries being sensitive to conversational context — the same predicate carves the world differently depending on where the conversation has taken the boundary.
- **Derivative order justification**: d1 because the category boundary itself has a trajectory — it moves through the sorites series or across conversational contexts. The categories (tall/not-tall, heap/not-heap) persist as d0 structures. The mechanism that drives the drift (contextual salience, conversational dynamics) would be d2. The first-order pattern is: boundaries drift.
- **Source**: `ocp:sep/vagueness` (trust: 0.559), `ocp:sep/sorites-paradox` (trust: 0.619), `ocp:sep/boundary` (trust: 0.633)
- **Key references**: Graff/Fara (2000) "Shifting Sands: An Interest-Relative Theory of Vagueness"; Raffman (2014) *Unruly Words*; Shapiro (2006) *Vagueness in Context*; Williamson (1994) *Vagueness*; Kamp (1981) "The Paradox of the Heap"

### Instance 3: Biology — Species Boundary Migration and Evo-Devo

- **Domain**: Evolutionary Biology / Taxonomy
- **Process**: Species boundaries drift under selection pressure. Ring species (e.g., *Ensatina eschscholtzii* salamanders, greenish warblers around the Himalayas) demonstrate boundary drift concretely: populations that interbreed at one end of a geographic ring cannot interbreed at the other end, with the species boundary drifting continuously around the ring. The SEP "Evolution and Development" entry documents how developmental modules create "zones of variational accessibility" — the boundary between what an organism can and cannot become drifts as developmental constraints shift under selection. The SEP "Macroevolution" entry (trust: 0.677) covers anagenesis (within-lineage change) where the boundary between ancestral and descendant morphotypes drifts continuously without speciation events. This is distinct from cladogenesis (branching), which creates new categories (d0) or changes classification criteria (d3). Anagenetic drift is pure d1: the same species boundary, moving through morphospace over time.
- **Derivative order justification**: d1 because the species/morphotype boundary is moving along a continuous trajectory. The classification system (species concept) stays fixed (d0). The mechanisms (selection, drift, developmental constraint) are d2. The first-order observable is: boundaries between species shift incrementally over geological time.
- **Source**: `ocp:sep/macroevolution` (trust: 0.677), `ocp:sep/evolution-development` (trust: 0.614), `ocp:sep/evolution` (trust: 0.571)
- **Key references**: Irwin et al. (2001) ring species; West-Eberhard (2003) *Developmental Plasticity and Evolution*; Mayr (1942) biological species concept; SEP "Philosophy of Macroevolution"

### Instance 4: Cultural Anthropology — Cultural Category Migration

- **Domain**: Cultural Evolution / Anthropology
- **Process**: Cultural categories drift under social learning dynamics. The SEP "Cultural Evolution" entry (trust: 0.635, bibliography: 128) documents how cultural traits — norms, classifications, word meanings — undergo directed change through biased transmission. Sperber's (1996) "cultural attractors" show that cultural categories (e.g., what counts as "food", "music", "appropriate behaviour") drift toward attractor points in conceptual space. The boundary between "music" and "noise" has drifted continuously over the history of Western art music (from Gregorian chant to Cage's 4'33"). Language change exemplifies this concretely: the boundary between "acceptable" and "unacceptable" grammatical forms drifts steadily (split infinitives, singular "they"). Darwin himself noted that language change is "natural selection" of word forms — the boundary between current and archaic vocabulary drifts.
- **Derivative order justification**: d1 because the cultural category boundary is moving along a trajectory. The existence of the category (music/noise, acceptable/unacceptable) is d0. The transmission biases and attractor dynamics that drive the drift are d2. The first-order observable is: cultural boundaries migrate over generations.
- **Source**: `ocp:sep/evolution-cultural` (trust: 0.635)
- **Key references**: Boyd & Richerson (1985, 2005) cultural evolutionary theory; Sperber (1996) *Explaining Culture*; Cladiere & Sperber (2007) "The Role of Attraction in Cultural Evolution"; Kelly et al. (2021) on predictable evolution of writing systems; Darwin (1871) on language change as natural selection

### Instance 5: Software Engineering — Schema Evolution and Migration

- **Domain**: Software Engineering / Data Management
- **Process**: Database schemas undergo boundary drift when the distinction between data types, field meanings, or entity categories shifts incrementally over the life of a system. Schema migration is the formalised practice of tracking these boundary movements. A "user" table gains a "role" field; the boundary between "admin" and "user" shifts; nullable fields drift toward required or deprecated; enum values expand or contract. API versioning (v1, v2, v3) documents the trajectory of type boundaries through time. The pattern is not schema replacement (that would be a rewrite, d2/d3) but incremental boundary displacement: each migration is a small shift in where distinctions fall. The GitHub scrape returned general ecosystem resources (`ocp:github/avelino--awesome-go`, `ocp:github/binhnguyennus--awesome-scalability`) that document this pattern indirectly through the prevalence of migration tooling in every mature ecosystem.
- **Derivative order justification**: d1 because the type/field/entity boundaries are moving incrementally over time. The schema framework (relational model, entity-relationship model) stays fixed (d0). The pressures that drive migration (new requirements, performance, regulatory change) are d2. The first-order observable is: data classification boundaries shift through successive migrations.
- **Source**: Domain knowledge + weak GitHub signal. No single OCP record directly captures schema-evolution-as-boundary-drift, though the pattern is ubiquitous in software practice.
- **Key references**: Ambler & Sadalage (2006) *Refactoring Databases*; Flyway, Liquibase, Alembic (migration tools); Roddick (1995) survey of schema evolution; API versioning practices (Stripe, GitHub, Kubernetes)

### Structural Coherence

The pattern is structurally coherent across all 5 instances: in each case, an existing classification boundary undergoes directed, incremental displacement over time without the classification framework itself being replaced. The key distinguishing features:

1. **Boundary exists prior to drift** (presupposes d0 distinctions)
2. **Displacement is directed** — not random fluctuation but a trajectory
3. **Framework persists** — the *kind* of distinction remains; only *where* it falls changes
4. **Incremental** — each step is small; the drift is visible only over accumulated steps

### Differentiation from Adjacent Orders

- **Not D/d0**: d0 motifs describe static distinctions (a classification exists). Boundary Drift describes distinctions *in motion*.
- **Not D/d2**: d2 motifs describe what *generates* the change in distinctions (plugin architecture that enables reconfiguration, state machines that govern transitions). Boundary Drift describes the first-order *trajectory* of the boundary, not the mechanism driving it.
- **Not D/d3**: d3 motifs (Paradigmatic Boundary Revision) describe changes to *what counts as a valid classification axis*. Boundary Drift operates within a fixed classification axis — only the boundary position moves.

### Adversarial Challenge

The primary risk: **"Is this just 'things change'?"** If Boundary Drift collapses to the trivial observation that categories are not static, it lacks structural content.

**Defence**: Boundary Drift is not merely "change happens". It specifies:
- The *unit* that changes (a classification boundary, not an object or a property)
- The *mode* of change (incremental displacement, not replacement or creation)
- The *observability* condition (the drift traces a trajectory; you can measure direction and rate)
- The *persistence* condition (the framework survives; only boundary position changes)

These four conditions exclude many forms of change. Revolution (framework replacement) is excluded. Creation of new categories is excluded. Random fluctuation without trajectory is excluded. The motif captures a specific structural shape: directed boundary migration within a persistent classification system.

**Falsification conditions**:
1. If no domain exhibits boundary drift distinct from framework replacement, the motif is empty
2. If the "trajectory" condition cannot be operationalised (no measurable direction), the motif degenerates to noise
3. If every instance reduces to "selection pressure causes change" (i.e., the d2 mechanism is the real pattern), then Boundary Drift is not a distinct d1 motif but a shadow of d2
4. If the boundary/framework distinction cannot be maintained (every boundary shift entails framework change), d1 collapses into d2/d3
