### Instance 3: Genetics — Genotype / Phenotype Mapping

- **Domain:** Molecular Biology / Genetics
- **Expression:** The genotype (DNA sequence) is the compact generative level; the phenotype (observable traits — morphology, behaviour, biochemistry) is the surface level. The genotype is never directly observed in the organism's outward form — only its phenotypic effects are. The mapping is many-to-many: a single genotype produces different phenotypes depending on environment, epigenetic state, and developmental context (phenotypic plasticity), while different genotypes can converge on the same phenotype (genetic redundancy, convergent evolution at the molecular level). The genotype-phenotype map is itself a structural object studied independently (e.g., fitness landscapes, epistasis networks).
- **Structural alignment:** Compact generative level (genotype) -> transformation rules (gene expression, splicing, post-translational modification, environmental interaction) -> diverse surface forms (phenotypes). Neither level reduces to the other. The mapping is the central object of quantitative genetics.
- **Confidence:** 0.85 — textbook-grade structural match with strong many-to-many character.

### Instance 4: Quantum Mechanics — Wavefunction / Measurement Outcome

- **Domain:** Physics / Quantum Mechanics
- **Expression:** The quantum state (wavefunction or density matrix) is the generative level; measurement outcomes (detector clicks, pointer positions, spectral lines) are the surface level. The wavefunction is never directly observed — only its statistical predictions for measurement outcomes are accessible. A single quantum state produces different measurement outcomes depending on which observable is measured (basis dependence), and different quantum states can yield identical statistics for a given observable (degeneracy, mixed states). The Born rule mapping between state and outcome probabilities is itself a fundamental structural object. Scientific realism debates (SEP: ocp:sep/scientific-realism) centre precisely on whether the generative level is "real" or merely instrumental — a meta-question about the motif itself.
- **Structural alignment:** Compact generative level (state vector in Hilbert space) -> transformation/measurement rules (projective measurement, POVM) -> diverse surface forms (outcome distributions). The generative level is informationally richer than any single surface observation. Tomographic reconstruction requires many surface-level measurements to infer the generative state.
- **Confidence:** 0.80 — strong structural match; the "never directly observed" criterion is definitional in QM. Slight caution: interpretive debates about wavefunction ontology add complexity but do not break the structural shape.

### Instance 5: Plate Tectonics — Mantle Convection / Surface Geology

- **Domain:** Earth Science / Geology
- **Expression:** Mantle convection currents and deep lithospheric dynamics are the generative level; surface geology (mountain ranges, rift valleys, volcanic arcs, earthquake distributions) is the surface level. The mantle dynamics are never directly observed — only their surface effects are (seismology provides indirect inference, not direct observation). A single convective regime produces diverse surface expressions depending on plate boundary geometry and crustal composition. Different deep configurations can produce superficially similar surface features (e.g., volcanic activity from both hotspots and subduction). The coupling between deep dynamics and surface expression is itself a studied structural object (geodynamic modelling).
- **Structural alignment:** Compact generative level (mantle flow patterns) -> transformation rules (plate boundary mechanics, crustal response) -> diverse surface forms (topography, volcanism, seismicity). The generative level operates on longer timescales and larger spatial scales than the surface manifestations.
- **Confidence:** 0.70 — good structural match, but the mapping is less cleanly many-to-many than genetics or QM. The generative level is more continuous/diffuse than discrete, which slightly weakens the "compact" criterion.
