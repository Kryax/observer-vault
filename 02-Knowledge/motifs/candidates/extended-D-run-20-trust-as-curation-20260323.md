## Domain 1: Linguistics — Lexical Adoption and Register Stratification

### Specific Instance

Languages continuously incorporate new vocabulary — borrowings from other languages, neologisms, slang, technical jargon. The process by which a word moves from "foreign/novel/marginal" to "nativised/standard/canonical" maps structurally to trust-as-curation.

**Borrowing and nativisation.** When English encounters a foreign word, it does not binary-accept or binary-reject it. The word enters a graduated status hierarchy:

1. **Foreign word** (italicised in print, explained in context): *Zeitgeist*, *schadenfreude* in early English use. Zero trust — the word must prove its utility.
2. **Established borrowing** (no longer italicised, used without explanation): *kindergarten*, *restaurant*. Moderate trust — demonstrated utility across enough contexts and speakers.
3. **Fully nativised** (morphologically adapted, speakers unaware of foreign origin): *beef* (from Norman French *boeuf*), *skill* (from Old Norse *skil*). Full trust — the word has been curated into the core lexicon.

This is not a one-time decision. Words earn their status through sustained use across diverse contexts and speaker communities. *Sushi* spent decades as an exotic italicised term before becoming unmarked English. *Tsunami* was a specialist term until the 2004 Indian Ocean event demonstrated its communicative utility, accelerating its nativisation.

**Slang and register climbing.** Slang terms start at the lowest trust level — informal, stigmatised, excluded from formal registers. Some earn promotion through demonstrated communicative utility: *cool* (jazz slang, 1930s) is now standard English across all registers. *OK* (1830s abbreviation craze) is arguably the most successful English word globally. Others fail to earn trust and die: *groovy*, *rad*, *tubular* — temporarily prominent, never promoted to standard register.

The lexicon is curated by this trust mechanism. Dictionaries function as explicit trust registries: inclusion in a major dictionary (OED, Merriam-Webster) is a formal recognition of earned trust, and entries carry graduated status markers ("informal," "archaic," "dialectal," "standard") that map to trust levels.

**Corpus frequency as implicit trust metric.** Computational linguistics provides direct measurement: word frequency in formal corpora (academic papers, legislation, broadsheet journalism) vs. informal corpora (social media, transcribed speech) reveals the trust gradient. A word frequent in informal but absent from formal registers is low-trust. A word frequent across all registers is high-trust. This gradient is continuous and changes over time as words earn or lose credibility.

### Structural Test

| Test | Result | Evidence |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1. Trust EARNED through demonstrated reliability? | **YES** | Words earn lexical status through sustained use demonstrating communicative utility. No authority grants a word admission to the lexicon — even Academie Francaise prescriptions are routinely overridden by usage (French speakers use *le weekend* despite official disapproval). |
| 2. Trust CONTINUOUS (not binary)? | **YES** | The register system is graduated: taboo < slang < informal < neutral < formal < literary/technical. Dictionary status markers are ordinal. Corpus frequency is continuous. Words occupy positions across this gradient, not binary in/out. |
| 3. Trust determines CURATION? | **YES** | This is genuine curation. The lexicon is not a collection of all words ever uttered — it is a curated system where trust (demonstrated utility over time) determines what enters the standard vocabulary, what register it occupies, and how prominently it features in formal language. Low-trust words exist but are excluded from formal registers. High-trust words are the default vocabulary. The system boundary (the "standard lexicon") is actively maintained by trust. |
| 4. Replaces binary access control? | **YES** | There is no binary gate for lexical entry. Words are not voted in or officially approved (prescriptivist attempts to impose binary gates — "this is not a real word" — systematically fail). Instead, the graduated trust mechanism curates entry and prominence. The transition from prescriptivism (binary: correct/incorrect) to descriptivism (graduated: usage determines status) in modern linguistics IS the structural transition from binary access control to trust-as-curation. |
| 5. Gameability (falsification)? | **YES — partially triggered** | Marketing coinages attempt to game lexical adoption: *synergy*, *disrupt*, *pivot* gain surface frequency through corporate repetition without genuine communicative novelty. Some succeed (*brand* as verb). Most fail — they achieve temporary register presence but are recognised as jargon and stratified accordingly. Propaganda language is a more serious gaming vector: totalitarian regimes forcibly inject vocabulary (*Newspeak* is Orwell's formalisation of this concern). When gaming is cheap (authority-imposed vocabulary), the curation function degrades. When gaming is costly (a word must earn genuine multi-context utility), the function holds. |

### Assessment

**Rating: STRONG (0.80)**

Linguistics provides a remarkably clean TaC instance. All five structural tests pass without qualification. The critical "curation" dimension — where TaC is weakest in several existing instances — is genuinely strong here. The lexicon is paradigmatically a curated system: words do not merely exist or not exist; they occupy positions in a graduated hierarchy of credibility, and that hierarchy is earned through demonstrated utility, not assigned by authority.

**Does "curation" genuinely apply?** Yes — this is one of the cleanest curation instances in the entire TaC inventory. The lexicon is literally curated by trust. Dictionaries are explicit curation instruments. Register stratification is a curation mechanism. The system boundary (standard vs. non-standard vocabulary) is maintained through graduated earned credibility. This is not "selection" (a one-time choice) or "weighting" (a numerical adjustment) — it is ongoing curation of a knowledge system through accumulated trust. The structural isomorphism with the Economics/Market Reputation instance is striking: just as eBay curates seller visibility through earned reputation scores, the language system curates lexical prominence through earned usage credibility.

**Key structural insight:** The prescriptivism-to-descriptivism transition in linguistics history is itself evidence that binary access control (prescriptivist "correct/incorrect" judgments) is structurally unstable when the entities being curated (words) have variable and learnable reliability. Descriptivism — the observation that usage determines status — is the linguistic community independently discovering that trust-as-curation is the stable architecture.

**Domain novelty:** HIGH — Linguistics shares no intellectual heritage with any existing TaC domain.

## Domain 2: Ecology — Mycorrhizal Network Resource Allocation

### Specific Instance

Mycorrhizal networks are underground fungal networks connecting the root systems of multiple plants. The fungal partner (mycobiont) provides mineral nutrients (especially phosphorus) to plant partners; the plant partners provide photosynthetically fixed carbon to the fungus. This mutualism connects ~90% of terrestrial plant species.

The key question for TaC: is resource allocation in mycorrhizal networks governed by earned trust (demonstrated reciprocity), or is it simple market exchange (immediate tit-for-tat)?

**Kiers et al. 2011 (Science): "Reciprocal Rewards Stabilize Cooperation in the Mycorrhizal Symbiosis."** This landmark study used radioactive tracers to demonstrate that mycorrhizal fungi preferentially allocate phosphorus to plant roots that provide more carbon, and plants preferentially allocate carbon to fungal partners that provide more phosphorus. Crucially, the allocation is **not binary** — partners that provide less are not cut off entirely but receive proportionally less. The allocation gradient is continuous.

**Fellbaum et al. 2014: nutrient exchange is conditional and graduated.** Further work showed that the fungal network can distinguish between plant partners providing different levels of carbon and adjusts phosphorus delivery accordingly. The fungal network has no central controller — the graduated allocation emerges from local biochemical signalling at each root-fungal interface.

**Bever et al. 2009: preferential allocation creates "biological markets."** The theoretical framework interprets mycorrhizal networks as biological markets where partners compete for mutualistic services. Plants "shop" for the best fungal partner; fungi "shop" for the best plant partner. The most reliable partners (measured by resource delivery) receive the most favourable exchange terms.

**The memory question.** The critical structural question is whether the mycorrhizal network accumulates trust over time (memory) or merely responds to current resource flows (instantaneous exchange). If allocation is purely instantaneous — the fungus delivers phosphorus proportional to current carbon receipt — this is a market, not a trust system. Trust requires temporal accumulation: past reliability influencing present treatment.

Evidence on memory is mixed:

- Seedlings connected to established mycorrhizal networks grow faster than seedlings paired with naive fungi (Simard et al. 1997), suggesting the network retains something from previous interactions.
- However, the mechanism may be physiological (a well-fed, established fungal network is simply a better partner) rather than informational (the fungus "remembers" which partners were reliable).
- Kiers's sanctions mechanism operates on timescales of days to weeks — substantially longer than instantaneous exchange but substantially shorter than the multi-year partnerships in the network. This intermediate timescale is ambiguous: it could reflect accumulated trust (reliability assessed over multiple exchange episodes) or delayed market adjustment (prices take time to equilibrate).

### Structural Test

| Test | Result | Evidence |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1. Trust EARNED through demonstrated reliability? | **PARTIAL** | Resource allocation tracks partner reliability (plants that provide more carbon get more phosphorus). But "reliability" may be better described as "current productivity" — there is limited evidence that past reliability matters independent of current resource flows. The earning dimension is present for current performance but unconfirmed for accumulated trust. |
| 2. Trust CONTINUOUS (not binary)? | **YES** | Allocation is clearly graduated. Kiers et al. showed continuous, proportional resource exchange — not binary connected/disconnected but graduated more/less. This is well-established. |
| 3. Trust determines CURATION? | **WEAK** | The network does not curate "what enters" in any clear sense. Any compatible root system that contacts the fungal network can form a connection. What varies is the intensity of resource exchange within established connections. This is resource *allocation* within an existing partnership, not *curation* of system membership or prominence. The fungus does not decide which plants to include in its network based on earned credibility — it responds to current resource signals at each interface. |
| 4. Replaces binary access control? | **PARTIAL** | There is no binary gate to network membership (physical contact suffices). But there was never a binary access control system to replace — the graduated allocation did not emerge as a transition from binary inclusion/exclusion. The system was always a mutualism with variable exchange rates. TaC describes a structural *transition* from binary to graduated; mycorrhizal networks never had the binary stage. |
| 5. Gameability (falsification)? | **YES — partially triggered** | Cheating is well-documented: some plant species (myco-heterotrophs like *Monotropa uniflora*, Indian Pipe) parasitise mycorrhizal networks, extracting carbon from the fungus without photosynthetic reciprocation. The sanctions mechanism (reduced allocation to non-reciprocators) is the anti-gaming defence. However, cheaters persist — the gaming is not fully resisted, suggesting the "trust" system is leaky. |

### Assessment

**Rating: WEAK (0.45)**

Mycorrhizal networks exhibit graduated resource allocation based on partner productivity, which maps to the "continuous" and "earned" dimensions of TaC. However, the critical "curation" dimension fails. The network does not curate membership or prominence — it adjusts resource exchange rates within existing partnerships. This is market exchange with graduated pricing, not trust-based curation of system composition.

**Does "curation" genuinely apply?** No — this is the critical failure point. Mycorrhizal resource allocation is better described as **market exchange** or **conditional cooperation** than as curation. The fungal network does not maintain a system boundary governed by trust. It does not decide "what enters and how prominently it features." It adjusts the terms of trade with existing partners based on current performance. The distinction matters: curation implies an agent or mechanism that governs system composition; market exchange is a bilateral transaction that adjusts mechanically to supply and demand signals.

**Comparison with Economics/Market Reputation instance.** The Economics instance (eBay, FICO) passes the curation test because the platform actively curates seller visibility — the platform's search algorithm uses trust scores to determine what buyers see. The mycorrhizal network has no equivalent curation layer. There is no "search algorithm" that surfaces reliable partners more prominently. There is only bilateral adjustment of resource flows at each interface. The absence of a system-level curation mechanism distinguishes market exchange from trust-as-curation.

**The memory problem is decisive.** Without confirmed temporal accumulation (past reliability influencing present treatment independent of current resource flows), the "earned" dimension of TaC is weakened to "currently-demonstrated." A system where trust is purely a function of current performance, with no memory of past reliability, is a spot market, not a trust system.

**Key structural insight for TaC:** This domain usefully sharpens the curation boundary. TaC requires a system-level curation mechanism — something that governs composition/prominence at the system level, not merely bilateral adjustment. Mycorrhizal networks fail this test, which is honest and informative. Not every graduated-allocation system is TaC. The curation dimension is the structural differentiator.

**Domain novelty:** HIGH — Ecology is genuinely alien to all existing TaC domains.

## Domain 3: Jurisprudence — Common Law Precedent Weighting

### Specific Instance

Common law systems (England, US, Canada, Australia, most of the Anglosphere) are built on the principle of *stare decisis* — the obligation to follow prior judicial decisions. But the structural reality is far more graduated than the naive model ("precedent is either binding or not") suggests.

**The graduated authority hierarchy.** Judicial precedent carries different levels of authority depending on multiple trust-like factors:

1. **Court level.** Supreme Court/High Court decisions bind lower courts (vertical binding authority). But even here, the influence is not binary — a Supreme Court decision on a narrow procedural point carries less practical weight than a Supreme Court decision establishing a broad constitutional principle, despite both being formally "binding."

2. **Citation frequency.** Precedents that are frequently cited by subsequent courts accumulate authority. A heavily cited precedent becomes a "leading case" — it earns that status through demonstrated applicability across many fact patterns and jurisdictions. Citation counts in legal databases (Westlaw, LexisNexis) function as implicit trust metrics. A case cited 500 times carries more practical weight than a case cited 5 times at the same court level.

3. **Persuasive authority gradient.** Decisions from other jurisdictions, lower courts, or dissenting opinions carry "persuasive authority" — they are not binding but can influence a judge's reasoning. The persuasive weight is graduated and earned: a well-reasoned opinion from a respected foreign court (e.g., UK Supreme Court cited in Australian cases) carries more persuasive weight than an unremarkable lower court decision from a neighbouring jurisdiction. This is trust earned through demonstrated analytical quality, not granted by hierarchical position.

4. **The distinguishing mechanism.** When a precedent proves unreliable (its reasoning does not generalise well), courts "distinguish" it — they narrow its scope by identifying factual differences that limit its applicability. This is trust attenuation, not binary removal. A distinguished precedent is still formally valid but its practical influence shrinks. Over time, a heavily distinguished precedent becomes effectively inoperative — death by a thousand qualifications — while remaining formally alive. This is graduated loss of trust without binary revocation.

5. **Overruling as the binary exception.** Outright overruling (a higher court declaring a precedent no longer good law) is rare and dramatic — it is the binary access control mechanism that the trust-as-curation system normally makes unnecessary. The system curates through graduated authority and distinguishing, resorting to binary removal only when the graduated system fails to adequately contain a bad precedent.

**The Restatements and treatises layer.** Legal scholarship adds another curation layer: the American Law Institute's Restatements synthesise case law into principle statements, and the authority of these statements is earned through demonstrated accuracy in predicting how courts will rule. A Restatement section that consistently aligns with judicial outcomes accumulates influence; one that courts reject loses it. Treatises by respected scholars (Prosser on torts, Williston on contracts) similarly earn authority through demonstrated analytical reliability, not through official endorsement.

### Structural Test

| Test | Result | Evidence |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1. Trust EARNED through demonstrated reliability? | **YES** | Precedents earn authority through demonstrated applicability across cases. Citation frequency is an empirical measure of earned trust. Persuasive authority is earned through analytical quality. Restatements earn authority through predictive accuracy. No precedent earns authority by decree — even Supreme Court decisions must prove their reasoning generalises to earn leading-case status. |
| 2. Trust CONTINUOUS (not binary)? | **YES** | The authority spectrum runs from "leading case, universally cited" through "frequently cited," "occasionally cited," "rarely cited," "distinguished," "doubted," to "effectively overruled in practice." This is a rich gradient. Citation counts are continuous. Persuasive authority is explicitly graduated ("highly persuasive," "somewhat persuasive," "of limited persuasive value"). |
| 3. Trust determines CURATION? | **YES** | Legal doctrine is curated by earned authority. The body of "good law" on any topic is not all cases ever decided — it is the subset that has earned citation authority and proven applicability. Headnote systems, case annotations, and digests explicitly curate the accessible law based on citation authority and subsequent treatment. Westlaw's "KeyCite" and LexisNexis's "Shepard's Citations" are literal curation systems that use earned authority (citation patterns, subsequent treatment) to curate which precedents a lawyer should rely on. |
| 4. Replaces binary access control? | **YES** | The structural transition is visible in legal history. Early common law was more rigidly binary (precedent is binding or it is not; departure from precedent requires formal overruling). Modern common law has evolved a rich graduated system where distinguishing, narrowing, expanding, and persuasive authority create a continuous influence gradient that makes binary overruling the exception rather than the rule. The system evolved away from binary access control toward graduated trust-based curation of legal authority. |
| 5. Gameability (falsification)? | **YES — partially triggered** | Forum shopping: litigants game the trust system by filing in jurisdictions with favourable precedent, exploiting the variation in precedential authority across jurisdictions. String citations: advocates inflate the apparent authority of weak precedents by citing them alongside strong ones. Law review placement gaming: articles placed in prestigious journals earn citation authority through the journal's reputation rather than the article's analytical quality. However, the system resists gaming through judicial scrutiny (courts routinely dismiss string citations and evaluate precedent quality independently) and through the slow, distributed nature of the trust-accumulation mechanism — gaming one case's citation count requires convincing many independent judges to cite it, which is inherently costly. |

### Assessment

**Rating: STRONG (0.85)**

Jurisprudence provides what may be the strongest TaC instance found across any domain, including Economics. All five structural tests pass cleanly. The curation dimension is not merely present — the common law system IS a trust-as-curation architecture. Legal doctrine is curated by earned reliability. The body of good law is not a fixed canon but a living system where precedents earn and lose authority based on demonstrated applicability.

**Does "curation" genuinely apply?** Emphatically yes. This is perhaps the purest curation instance in the entire TaC inventory. The common law system curates legal doctrine — it maintains a structured body of authoritative principles, with entries (precedents) ranked by earned authority, new entries admitted through demonstrated utility, and failing entries attenuated through distinguishing. Legal databases (Westlaw KeyCite, Shepard's Citations) are explicit, purpose-built curation systems that organise law by earned authority. The structural isomorphism with the motif's formulation is near-perfect: "continuous credibility earned through demonstrated reliability determines what enters the system [body of good law] and how prominently it persists [citation authority, leading-case status], replacing binary inclusion/exclusion [overruling] with graduated influence [persuasive authority, distinguishing]."

**Key structural insight:** The distinguishing mechanism is a structural feature unique to this domain that illuminates TaC's architecture. In most TaC instances, trust attenuation is a reduction in a numeric score. In common law, trust attenuation is *qualitative* — a precedent's scope is narrowed through reasoned argument, limiting the contexts in which it applies. This shows that TaC's "graduated influence" need not be numeric; it can be scope-based. The precedent still exists but applies to fewer situations — its domain of influence contracts. This enriches the structural understanding of what "graduated influence" means across domains.

**Comparison with the T2 validation prediction.** The T2 validation (extended-A-run-5) predicted this domain as the strongest untested avenue. The prediction is confirmed and arguably exceeded — the structural match is tighter than anticipated, particularly in the distinguishing mechanism (which maps to trust attenuation) and the explicit curation infrastructure (KeyCite, Shepard's).

**Domain novelty:** HIGH — Jurisprudence shares no intellectual heritage with any existing TaC domain.

## Domain 4: Neuroscience — Bayesian Predictive Processing / Precision Weighting

### Specific Instance

The predictive processing framework (Friston 2005, 2010; Clark 2013, 2015) proposes that the brain is a prediction machine: it continuously generates predictions about incoming sensory input, compares predictions against actual input, and updates its generative model based on prediction errors. The core computational mechanism is precision-weighted prediction error minimisation.

**Precision weighting as earned credibility.** In predictive processing, every prediction and every sensory signal carries a "precision" estimate — a measure of expected reliability. Precision is inverse variance: high precision means the signal is expected to be reliable (low variance); low precision means it is expected to be unreliable (high variance).

Crucially, precision is not fixed. It is updated through experience:

- A prior (top-down prediction) that consistently generates accurate predictions earns higher precision. The brain treats it as increasingly reliable.
- A prior that generates frequent prediction errors loses precision. The brain downgrades its influence.
- A sensory channel that provides consistent, low-noise input earns higher precision. The brain weights it more heavily.
- A sensory channel in a noisy environment loses precision. The brain downgrades it in favour of stronger priors.

This is earned credibility: priors and sensory channels accumulate or lose influence based on demonstrated predictive accuracy.

**Precision determines perceptual dominance.** The precision-weighted competition between priors and sensory evidence determines what reaches conscious perception. A high-precision prior can override low-precision sensory input (as in top-down illusions: you see what you expect, not what is there, when sensory input is ambiguous). Conversely, a strong sensory signal (high precision) can override a prior expectation (as in surprise: something unexpected breaks through the predictive model).

This competition is not binary — it is a continuous weighted mixture. Perception is the precision-weighted combination of prior expectation and sensory evidence. The "winner" of any perceptual moment is determined by relative precision — earned credibility — not by a binary gate.

**Attention as precision optimisation.** In predictive processing, attention IS precision weighting. To attend to something is to increase the precision (expected reliability) of sensory signals in that channel. Attention curates perception by selectively boosting the credibility of certain sensory streams and suppressing others. This is a trust-like mechanism: attended sensory channels are "trusted" more; unattended channels are "trusted" less.

**Pathological cases: when precision weighting fails.** Aberrant precision weighting is proposed as a unifying explanation for multiple psychiatric conditions:

- **Schizophrenia (hallucinations):** Overweighted priors — the brain assigns too much precision to internal predictions, which override sensory evidence. The generative model "trusts" its own predictions too much. The curation function fails: internally generated content enters perception at inappropriately high prominence.
- **Autism:** Overweighted sensory input — the brain assigns too much precision to raw sensory data, failing to use priors to smooth and predict. Every sensory detail receives attention, overwhelming the system.
- **Anxiety:** Overweighted threat priors — the brain assigns too much precision to predictions of danger, biasing perception toward threat detection.

These pathological cases are structurally analogous to the TaC gameability condition: when the precision-weighting mechanism misfires (trust is assigned incorrectly), the curation function fails in predictable ways.

### Structural Test

| Test | Result | Evidence |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1. Trust EARNED through demonstrated reliability? | **YES** | Precision is updated based on prediction accuracy. Priors that consistently generate accurate predictions earn higher precision. Sensory channels that provide consistent, low-noise input earn higher precision. No component earns credibility by authority — it earns it through track record. |
| 2. Trust CONTINUOUS (not binary)? | **YES** | Precision is a continuous real-valued quantity (inverse variance). The competition between priors and sensory evidence is a continuous weighted mixture, not a binary switch. Perceptual dominance is a graded function of relative precision. |
| 3. Trust determines CURATION? | **MODERATE** | Precision weighting determines what reaches conscious perception — which priors dominate, which sensory signals break through, what is attended and what is suppressed. This IS curation of perceptual content. But the "what enters the system" framing is strained: priors and sensory signals are not external entities seeking entry. They are internal components competing for influence within an already-existing system. The curation is of *prominence* and *influence*, not of *entry*. This is better described as "trust determines prominence" than "trust determines what enters." |
| 4. Replaces binary access control? | **MODERATE** | There is no prior binary access control system that precision weighting replaced. The brain did not evolve from a binary perception system to a graduated one (so far as we know). However, the structural claim holds in a functional sense: precision weighting provides graduated influence instead of binary signal selection. The alternative architecture (binary: attend or ignore, perceive or suppress) would be less adaptive. The graduated mechanism is structurally superior to a hypothetical binary alternative, even if the binary stage never historically existed. |
| 5. Gameability (falsification)? | **YES — partially triggered** | Hallucinations, delusions, anxiety disorders, and autism spectrum conditions all represent precision-weighting failures. The system assigns trust (precision) incorrectly — too much to priors (hallucinations), too much to sensory data (sensory overload), too much to threat predictions (anxiety). These are precisely the failure modes TaC predicts: when the trust-assignment mechanism misfires, the curation function fails. The psychiatric conditions are TaC falsification events in the neuroscience domain. |

### Assessment

**Rating: MODERATE (0.65)**

Predictive processing implements a genuine trust-as-curation mechanism in two of three structural dimensions: trust is earned through demonstrated predictive accuracy, and trust is continuous (precision is a real-valued quantity). The curation dimension is present but strained — precision weighting curates perceptual prominence and attentional allocation, but the "what enters the system" framing does not map cleanly because priors and sensory signals are internal components, not external entities seeking admission.

**Does "curation" genuinely apply?** Partially. Precision weighting curates the *contents of conscious perception* — what you see, hear, and feel is determined by a trust-weighted competition between predictions and sensory evidence. This is genuine curation of a system (conscious experience) through earned credibility (predictive accuracy). However, the curation is of *influence and prominence*, not of *entry*. The distinction matters: in the Economics and Jurisprudence instances, entities (sellers, precedents) genuinely seek entry into a system and are curated by trust. In the neuroscience instance, the components are already within the system and compete for influence through earned precision. This is closer to "trust-as-weighting" than "trust-as-curation" in the strict sense.

**The attention mapping saves the curation dimension.** If attention is understood as precision optimisation (Feldman & Friston 2010), then attention IS a curation mechanism: it selects which sensory streams are amplified and which are suppressed, governing what enters conscious experience. Attended channels "enter" perception; unattended channels are "excluded." This is closer to the TaC formulation — attention curates perceptual content through trust (precision) assigned by predictive accuracy. The curation is real but operates at the level of perceptual access rather than system membership.

**Key structural insight:** The pathological cases are particularly valuable for TaC. They provide a clean falsification test: when precision weighting misfires, the curation function fails in domain-specific but structurally predictable ways. Hallucinations = overweighted priors curating false perceptions into consciousness. Anxiety = overweighted threat priors curating danger signals into prominence they don't deserve. These map precisely to the TaC falsification condition: "if trust can be gamed easily [assigned incorrectly], the curation function fails [wrong content reaches prominence]." The brain is "gaming itself" through miscalibrated precision.

**Comparison with the T2 validation prediction.** The T2 validation predicted this domain as "borderline" — present for "earned credibility" and "graduated influence" but weak for "curation of what enters." This assessment is confirmed. The domain is better than borderline (the attention-as-curation mapping and the pathological falsification cases add structural depth) but weaker than the Jurisprudence or Linguistics instances on the curation dimension.

**Domain novelty:** HIGH — Neuroscience shares no intellectual heritage with any existing TaC domain.

### The Curation Test: Definitive Results

The four domains provide a clean gradient on the curation dimension, which is the structural differentiator between TaC and "reputation exists":

1. **Jurisprudence (0.85):** Curation is the primary function. The common law system IS a curation system for legal doctrine, governed by earned authority.
2. **Linguistics (0.80):** Curation is explicit. The lexicon IS a curated system. Dictionaries are curation instruments. Register stratification is a curation hierarchy.

This gradient sharpens TaC's scope condition: **TaC applies to systems where there IS a curated system with a meaningful boundary between "in" and "out" (or "prominent" and "marginal"). It does not apply to bilateral exchange systems where there is no system-level curation function.** The mycorrhizal failure is diagnostic: graduated resource allocation alone is not sufficient for TaC. The curation architecture — a system boundary maintained by trust — is the structurally necessary component.

### Weakest-Point Targeting: Results

**1. Curation dimension strain.** Confirmed as the decisive structural boundary. Two of four new domains (Linguistics, Jurisprudence) pass the curation test cleanly. One (Neuroscience) passes partially — curation of prominence rather than entry. One (Ecology) fails — no curation function present. The curation dimension is not an artifact; it is the structural core of TaC. Without it, the pattern collapses to "graduated allocation," which is too broad to be a useful structural operator.

**2. Dynamic equilibrium concern.** All four domains confirm TaC as a maintained pattern:
- Linguistics: lexical status is continuously renegotiated through usage patterns.
- Ecology: resource allocation adjusts in real time (this is why it's not TaC — it's too dynamic, with no accumulated memory).
- Jurisprudence: precedent authority evolves as new cases cite, distinguish, or extend prior decisions.
- Neuroscience: precision weights are continuously updated through prediction error.

TaC is not a static invariant. It is a dynamic equilibrium maintained by continuous trust assessment. This is neither a weakness nor a strength — it is a scope condition. TaC describes systems that maintain curated order through ongoing trust evaluation, not systems with static trust assignments.

**3. Scope condition test.** The ecology domain confirms the scope boundary: TaC requires (a) variable, learnable reliability, (b) a system boundary governed by trust, and (c) a curation function that determines composition/prominence. Systems with (a) but not (b) and (c) — like mycorrhizal networks — are bilateral exchange systems, not TaC instances.
