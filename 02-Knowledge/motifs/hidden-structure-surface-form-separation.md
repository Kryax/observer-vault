# Hidden Structure / Surface Form Separation

## Structural Description

A system operates on two levels: a compact generative level that encodes relationships and constraints, and an expanded surface level that is the observable output. Multiple distinct surface forms can map back to the same generative structure, and a single generative structure can produce multiple surface forms depending on context or transformation rules applied. The generative level is never directly observed — only its surface effects are. The two levels can be designed or analyzed independently, and the mapping between them is itself a structural object.

## Domain-Independent Formulation

A system with a compact generative level producing diverse observable surface forms, where the generative level is not directly observable and the mapping between levels is many-to-many.

## Instances

### Instance 1: Linguistics — Deep Structure / Surface Structure

- **Domain:** Linguistics / Generative Grammar
- **Expression:** A single deep structure (e.g., agent-action-patient relation) produces varied surface sentences through movement, deletion, and passivization. "The dog bit the man" and "The man was bitten by the dog" share a generative structure but differ at the surface. The deep structure is never directly spoken — only its surface transformations are.
- **Discovery date:** 2026-03-19
- **Source:** top-down

### Instance 2: Music Theory — Harmonic Function vs Chord Identity

- **Domain:** Music Theory
- **Expression:** Harmonic function (tonic, subdominant, dominant) is the generative level; specific chords are the surface level. A C major chord is "tonic" in C major but "subdominant" in G major — same surface entity, different generative role. The functional relationships generate the progressions; the specific chords are surface manifestations that can be freely substituted as long as function is preserved.
- **Discovery date:** 2026-03-19
- **Source:** top-down

## Discovery Context

Discovered during a slow triad exploring linguistics and music theory. Generative grammar's deep/surface structure distinction and harmonic function theory's role/identity distinction independently surfaced the same structural shape: two-level systems where the observable level does not directly reveal the generative level.

## Falsification Conditions

- If the two levels in supposed instances could be shown to always stand in a one-to-one mapping (each generative structure produces exactly one surface form, and each surface form maps to exactly one generative structure), the motif would be trivial — the "separation" would be mere relabeling.
- If the "generative level" in supposed instances is a post-hoc analytical fiction rather than a causally operative layer (i.e., removing it from the model changes no predictions), the motif would be decorative.
