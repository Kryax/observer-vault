# Nested Self-Similar Hierarchy

## Structural Description

A system organizes into levels where each level groups elements from the level below, and the relationships governing one level are structurally similar to those at adjacent levels. Boundaries at each level are marked by the same type of closure signal, differing only in magnitude. The system does not merely nest — it reapplies its own organizational principle at each scale. This produces fractal-like coherence: understanding the pattern at one level provides predictive power at all other levels.

## Domain-Independent Formulation

A system whose organizational principle is reapplied at each scale level, producing structurally similar dynamics from micro to macro.

## Instances

### Instance 1: Music Theory — Rhythmic and Formal Hierarchy
- **Domain:** Music Theory
- **Expression:** Beats group into measures, measures into phrases, phrases into sections, sections into movements. At each level there is a strong-weak alternation, and each level's grouping is marked by a cadential gesture appropriate to that scale. The macro form (sonata exposition-development-recapitulation) is recapitulated at micro scale within each section. Closure signals operate identically at every level, differing only in weight.

## Discovery Context

Discovered during a slow triad exploring music theory. Rhythmic hierarchy, formal nesting, and cadential hierarchy all exhibited the same structural property: the organizational principle at one level is the same principle reapplied at the next. This self-similar nesting was the clearest recurse-axis pattern in the music theory domain.

## Falsification Conditions

- If supposed instances are merely nested (hierarchy) without genuine self-similarity (same structural principle at each level), the motif would be a trivial observation about hierarchy rather than a structural pattern. The test: does understanding the level-N pattern predict the level-(N+1) pattern?
- If the self-similarity breaks down consistently at a particular scale boundary (e.g., the micro-level and macro-level use genuinely different organizing principles), the motif would be limited rather than universal.
