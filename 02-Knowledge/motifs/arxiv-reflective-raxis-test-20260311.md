- The archive appears to reward field-standard technical labels over meta-theoretical or cybernetic labels.
- As evidence, this scrape says more about substrate retrievability than about the motif itself.

### `cs.AI` search: `second-order cybernetics OR observer-dependent OR participatory`

- The zero-result outcome reinforces that observer-theoretic language is not a stable retrieval cluster inside `cs.AI`.
- Reflective terms may be present only after translation into mainstream technical discourse.
- The absence suggests arXiv's AI corpus operationalizes these concerns without preserving their philosophical names.
- This is a signal that recurse-axis search by reflective vocabulary may systematically underperform on arXiv.

### `physics.hist-ph`

- The main structural signal is second-order: not what mechanism produces a state, but what framing makes a claim about that state meaningful.
- Measurement, contextuality, decoherence, and quantum advantage all depend on observer stance, evidential criteria, and interpretive partitioning.
- In the quantum cosmology paper, removing time does not just alter formalism; it removes a class of meaningful predictions.
- In the chaos-history paper, epistemic doctrine (`positivism`) functioned as a filter that made some mathematical futures thinkable and others “meaningless.”
- In the probability-trajectory paper, different descriptions of process are not interchangeable; the frame changes what kind of evolution is being talked about.
- The recurse-axis signal is therefore strong, but conceptual rather than mechanistic: physics is interrogating the conditions of its own legibility.

### `cs.AI` search: `process philosophy OR enactivism OR embodied cognition`

- The zero-result outcome suggests that even relatively familiar embodied/participatory language does not form a retrievable reflective cluster here.
- This strengthens the substrate-bias hypothesis: reflective motifs may be filtered out by category and naming convention long before analysis begins.
- arXiv appears stronger at surfacing formal/mathematical recurse causes than phenomenological or process-philosophical framings.

- The previous arXiv test surfaced mechanism-heavy candidate structure: projections, filters, interfaces, coupling laws, boundary conditions.
- This reflective scrape surfaces something different: the observer, the interpretive frame, and the criterion of meaningful prediction become explicit variables.
- So yes, the R-axis signal looks qualitatively different when the source material is reflective rather than mechanistic.
- But the difference is not “cleaner operator separation”; it is “stronger visibility of epistemic recursion and weaker visibility of a buildable operator.”

### Do the recurring d-failures still hold?

- Yes.
- `RSR` remains unrescued.
- `AFR` is not cleanly separated by reflective evidence either, because the reflective corpus does not crystallize around a candidate-shaped technical family.
- Instead, the reflective material broadens the meta-level context while leaving the same promotion problem unresolved.

### Does the Tier 3 hypothesis get stronger or weaker?

- Stronger as a meta-hypothesis, weaker as a direct validation event.
- The reflective evidence suggests the recurse-axis gap may be compositional: not a standalone Tier 2 operator, but a higher-order relation among existing Tier 2 operators plus observer/epistemic framing.
- The reason it gets stronger is that reflective sources keep pointing to interactions between framing, gating, compression, and state structure rather than to one isolated missing mechanism.

### Is the substrate itself biasing the search?

- Yes, strongly.
- arXiv appears suitable for mechanistic recurse discovery when the field names the mechanism directly.
- arXiv appears poor for reflective recurse discovery when the discourse is cybernetic, participatory, phenomenological, or process-philosophical.
- That means the evidence substrate is likely biasing the search toward mechanism and away from meta-structure.
