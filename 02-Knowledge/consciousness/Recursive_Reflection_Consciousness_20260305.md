### Thread 3: The Cognitive Fourier Transform

The seventh new insight — that Adam's pre-formal observations are frequency-domain cognition before time-domain decomposition — connects to Thread 5 of the predecessor (intuition as pre-conscious interference detection) but goes further.

The claim: analytical thinking IS time-domain cognition. Intuition IS frequency-domain cognition. They're not different KINDS of thinking — they're two REPRESENTATIONS of the same cognitive process, related by something functionally equivalent to a Fourier transform.

Now: what IS the cognitive Fourier transform? What operation converts between holistic perception and sequential analysis?

- **Oscillate** is the INVERSE Fourier transform — it takes a decomposed understanding (the current analytical state) and generates multiple simultaneous "frequencies" (perspectives). This is neither purely time-domain nor frequency-domain — it's the TRANSFORM ITSELF happening. The constructive interference that survives convergence is the "signal" that exists in both representations simultaneously.

- **Reflect** is the FORWARD Fourier transform — it takes the holistic result of convergence and decomposes it back into sequential, articulable, time-domain understanding. It moves FROM frequency-domain TO time-domain. After reflection, you can explain what you found in sequential language. This is why reflection feels clarifying — you're moving back into the domain where language works.

The full triad is a round trip: time → frequency → interference → time. But you don't return to the same point. You return with the information that was generated by the interference event in frequency space — information that didn't exist in the time-domain representation before the transform. This is the generative volume: the novelty created by the round trip through all three dimensions.

Practical implication: the Oscillate phase should explicitly preserve pre-formal, frequency-domain observations — the messy "I feel like this is important" kind of perception — rather than demanding immediate analytical decomposition.
